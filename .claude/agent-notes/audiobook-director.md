# Field Notes -- audiobook-director

> Durable, verified lessons this agent has learned in its field. One lesson per entry, dated (ISO), with its source/citation. The charter (.claude/agents/audiobook-director.md) is the stable role; this file is the growing, citable knowledge. The author may prune.

- 2026-06-30: A narration script can silently go STALE when the manuscript is revised after the script was first tagged. On the Ch1 re-verify pass the existing `.narrative-script.md` still carried a pre-revision draft and diverged from the approved manuscript in ~15 places, including a fully DROPPED prose sentence ("We appreciate your continued cooperation." after the manufacturer wording, manuscript chapter-01-no-signal.md:161). Lesson: never trust an existing script's prose; on every pass re-diff the stripped script against the CURRENT manuscript token-for-token and watch specifically for whole dropped sentences inside the corporate-notice blocks (the highest-churn spots). Source: chapter-01-no-signal.md vs. its prior .narrative-script.md, 2026-06-30 re-verify.
- 2026-06-30: Common stale-drift points in Ch1 after manuscript revisions, useful as a re-check list for any chapter: opening-agency rewrites; "passed node to node"; "set down" vs "put down"; "half-open" hyphenation; cut empty intensifiers ("He stood still." not "very still"); the climax referents ("pointed at the correctness", "That was the wall.", "No bottom to it.", "the three machines" not "the three names"); reordered Lena lines ("...where the man is" lands last; "I'm not giving you their names, Eli"); and a removed stacked comma ("Eli?" not "Eli?,"). Source: chapter-01 Adjudication/Revision logs in chapter-01-no-signal.md.
