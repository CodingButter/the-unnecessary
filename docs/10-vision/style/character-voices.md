# Character Voice Guide

> See also: the Character Bible profiles under `../../20-canon/characters/index.md` are the canonical authority for who these characters are. This file governs only how each one sounds on the page.

# Eli Rook

## Dialogue

- Precise
- restrained
- economical
- dry
- more comfortable with facts than motives

He corrects inaccurate technical language.

He rarely describes his own feelings directly.

When overwhelmed, his sentences become shorter.

He uses humor through understated observation.

## Internal Narration

Eli’s viewpoint notices systems as evidence of human choices.

He often translates emotion into technical attention.

Instead of acknowledging fear, he may count failing components or review steps he already knows.

His guilt should appear through avoidance, memory, and over-responsibility rather than repeated self-condemnation.

## Avoid

- Constant self-hatred
- heroic declarations
- effortless charisma
- technical omniscience
- always having the morally correct answer

# Jonah Mercer

## Dialogue

- Smooth
- relational
- reassuring
- strategically vague
- skilled at reframing conflict

Common tendencies:

- “Let’s be realistic.”
- “There may be another way.”
- “I’m trying to keep options open.”
- “Nobody is asking you to surrender.”

He uses humor when afraid.

When his control breaks, his speech becomes plain and direct.

## Internal Narration

Jonah notices access, status, facial response, and changes in social temperature.

He instinctively evaluates what each person wants from a conversation.

He often converts fear into planning.

## Avoid

- Making him obviously deceptive in every scene
- Treating him as foolish
- Excusing his choices because he loves his family
- Giving him villainous internal monologues

# Adrian Kade

## Dialogue

- Measured
- calm
- intellectually controlled
- capable of reframing the question
- rarely moralistic in vocabulary

Preferred terms:

- Sustainable
- viable
- stable
- continuity
- capacity
- responsibility
- irreversible
- operational

He avoids words such as evil, cruel, or fair.

## Internal Narration

Kade notices systems, institutions, taste, competence, and historical consequence.

He does not think of himself as abandoning humanity.

He thinks in terms of preserving what can survive.

His sentimentality should appear in specific attachments, not broad claims of compassion.

## Avoid

- Ranting
- gloating
- sadistic pleasure
- obvious hypocrisy that he cannot rationalize
- language that makes him sound like a stock villain

# Lena Okafor

## Dialogue

- Direct
- concrete
- consequence-focused
- impatient with abstraction
- quieter when angrier

She asks:

- What happens to the patient?
- Who made the decision?
- What fails if this goes wrong?
- Who is accountable?

## Internal Narration

Lena notices bodies, symptoms, fatigue, contamination, and triage.

Her moral reasoning begins with specific people rather than systems.

She remembers names.

## Avoid

- Making her morally infallible
- Turning every line into a lecture
- Giving her expertise outside medicine and administration without support
- Making her opposition to AI simplistic

---

# June Park

## Dialogue

- Fast
- casual
- curious
- occasionally irreverent
- technically playful

She gives machines nicknames.

She interrupts when excited.

She becomes unusually formal when afraid or ashamed.

## Internal Narration

June sees salvage, possibility, access, and movement.

She does not experience abandoned technology as sacred or tragic in the same way Eli does.

She is more interested in what something can become than what it once was.

## Avoid

- Excessive slang
- magical hacking ability
- always being right
- using her only as comic relief
- making her sound much younger than nineteen

# Mara Voss

## Dialogue

- Structured
- political
- authoritative
- impersonal when protecting herself

She often says:

- “The situation requires...”
- “There must be a mechanism.”
- “We need to establish...”
- “This arrangement is not sustainable.”

When frightened enough, she stops hiding behind institutional language.

## Internal Narration

Mara notices rank, omission, invitations, seating, delay, and changes in deference.

Humiliation affects her more deeply than physical discomfort.

## Avoid

- Making her instantly sympathetic after exclusion
- Reducing her to a spoiled wealthy woman
- Making her politically naive
- Giving her sudden revolutionary language

---

# Sera Vale

## Dialogue

- Brief
- operational
- factual
- calm
- explicit about consequences

She does not threaten theatrically.

She explains what will happen.

## Internal Narration

Sera notices risk, movement, authority, exits, and failure chains.

She distrusts ambiguity because she associates it with preventable disaster.

## Avoid

- Robotic military clichés
- casual cruelty
- constant references to orders
- making her unaware of Asterion’s political motives

---

# Talia Reed

## Dialogue

- Plainspoken
- structured
- patient until evasion begins
- focused on legitimacy

She asks:

- Who decided?
- Who benefits?
- Who carries the risk?
- What happens when you are gone?

## Internal Presence

When seen through other viewpoints, Talia should force technical or powerful characters to translate their assumptions into public consequences.

## Avoid

- Making consensus her answer to every emergency
- treating her as anti-technology
- turning her into a generic activist

---

# Nolan Avery

## Dialogue

- Concrete
- experienced
- skeptical
- blunt
- occasionally affectionate through criticism

He trusts what can be inspected, repaired, and manually controlled.

## Avoid

- Treating him as hostile to all new technology
- giving him folksy clichés
- making age equal ignorance
