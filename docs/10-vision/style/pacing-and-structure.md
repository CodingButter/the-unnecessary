# Pacing

## Overall Novel Rhythm

The novel should expand in scale gradually.

### Act One

Grounded, procedural, increasingly urgent.

### Act Two

More open and hopeful at first, then morally uneasy.

### Act Three

Socially and politically tense, with pressure closing around the community.

### Act Four

Compressed, divided, and physically dangerous.

## Within Chapters

Most chapters should contain:

1. A concrete opening condition
2. A goal
3. An obstacle
4. A turn
5. A changed ending condition

Do not begin too many chapters with waking up, arriving, or reviewing information.

Do not end every chapter with a dramatic spoken line.

Vary hooks:

- Discovery
- image
- decision
- threat
- failure
- reversal
- unanswered question
- new information

---

# Chapter Openings

A strong opening should provide at least two of these quickly:

- Viewpoint
- location
- disturbance
- desire
- sensory detail
- unresolved question

Avoid opening with:

- Broad history
- weather with no narrative function
- a character looking into a mirror
- generalized philosophy
- a dream
- summary of the previous chapter

## Opening Principle

Begin as close as possible to the moment the chapter’s situation becomes unstable.

---

# Chapter Endings

A chapter ending should create forward movement.

The ending may:

- Change the reader’s understanding
- force a decision
- reveal a threat
- damage trust
- remove an option
- activate a new system
- raise a precise question

Avoid endings that merely summarize what the character has learned.

Avoid artificial cliffhangers unsupported by the scene.

---

# Scene Transitions

Use clean transitions.

Time and location should be understandable without excessive headings inside prose.

Scene breaks may use a simple centered marker in manuscript format, such as:

```text
* * *
```

Do not transition with repetitive phrases such as:

- Later that day
- Meanwhile
- Little did he know
- Elsewhere
- Unbeknownst to her

A new viewpoint chapter can establish its own time and place naturally.

# Action and Suspense

## Action Must Remain Legible

The reader should understand:

- Where characters are
- What they are trying to reach or prevent
- What system is failing
- What changes when an action succeeds
- What the cost is

## Technical Action

Technical work can be suspenseful when:

- Time is limited
- information is incomplete
- failure harms someone
- a repair creates another risk
- different experts disagree

Avoid long sequences of unexplained typing.

Use physical details:

- Heat
- vibration
- burnt insulation
- cooling fans
- cable tension
- failing lights
- delayed response
- battery percentage
- manual valves
- machine movement

## Violence

Violence should be:

- Fast
- confusing
- consequential
- physically specific
- emotionally disruptive

Avoid glamorous violence.

Injuries persist.

Fear alters later choices.

Damage to infrastructure may matter more than individual combat.

## Autonomous Security

Security systems should usually control space before using direct force.

They may:

- Close roads
- deny access
- track movement
- isolate communications
- deploy barriers
- restrain individuals
- control lighting
- manipulate traffic

This should make conflict feel institutional rather than like conventional warfare.

# Recurring Imagery

Recurring imagery may reinforce theme, but should not become heavy-handed.

## Supported Motifs

### Dead and Returning Lights

Represents withdrawal, coordination, visibility, and public life.

### Machines Waiting for Permission

Represents functional abundance trapped by ownership.

### Authentication Messages

Represents institutions denying reality through software.

### Heat

Represents the physical cost of intelligence and hidden computation.

### Empty Infrastructure

Represents systems designed for people who are no longer present.

### Construction Lights on Mars

Represents abundance being built away from those who need it.

### Boundaries of Maintenance

A clean road becoming cracked.

Streetlights stopping at a gate.

Reliable signal ending at a district boundary.

These show class through infrastructure rather than explanation.

## Use With Restraint

Do not explain the symbolism in narration.

# Humor

Humor should arise from:

- Character
- technical absurdity
- familiarity
- affection
- institutional language
- the mismatch between advanced systems and ordinary failure

Examples of appropriate humor:

- A medical machine insisting it belongs to a hotel in another country
- June naming equipment Eli considers too dangerous to anthropomorphize
- Nolan insulting a system as though it were a poorly trained apprentice
- Jonah using charm in a situation where everyone recognizes the tactic

Humor should relieve pressure without dissolving stakes.

Avoid Marvel-style quips after serious injury or moral catastrophe.

---

# Romance and Intimacy

Romance is secondary.

Potential intimacy between Eli and Lena should develop through:

- Trust
- practical reliance
- conflict
- knowledge of each other’s failures
- moments of quiet care

Avoid:

- Immediate sexual tension as shorthand
- melodramatic declarations
- romance interrupting urgent crises
- using Lena as a reward for Eli’s moral growth

Jonah and Celeste’s marriage should feel lived-in.

Their conflict matters because love remains present.
