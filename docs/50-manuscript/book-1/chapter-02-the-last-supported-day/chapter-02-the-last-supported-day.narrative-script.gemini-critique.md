## Medium Severity

- **Category:** Register Distinction
- **Location:** Multiple patient dialogue sections, e.g., `"You'd have had me scanned and stitched and out the door in an hour, time was," he said.`
- **Issue:** The script's excellent per-register direction notes (Reyes: dry, Diallo: tired/clear, Dembélé: satisfied/loss, Adeyemi: thin/wry) are flattened into a single generic `[plain]` tag for all patients. This misses a key opportunity for vocal texture and character differentiation.
- **Suggestion:** Replace the generic `[plain]` tag for each patient with a specific tag that reflects the script's own direction. For example: Reyes `[dry]`, Mrs. Diallo `[rueful]`, Dembélé `[satisfied]`, Mr. Adeyemi `[thin]`.
- **Severity:** Medium

- **Category:** Register Distinction
- **Location:** `"I took them." She put her hand on his shoulder a moment...`
- **Issue:** The voice direction notes mention a "tenderness at a bedside," but Lena's register tags (`weary`, `controlled`, `tense`) never reflect this. In moments of physical connection, like with Dembélé or holding Mr. Adeyemi's sleeve, the performance remains `[weary]`, which undercuts the warmth the prose describes.
- **Suggestion:** In key moments of physical, gentle contact, add a specific tag like `[gentle]` or `[warm]` to briefly shift Lena's register. For example: `[gentle] "I took them."`
- **Severity:** Medium

- **Category:** Fidelity
- **Location:** `...the link that afternoon dropping his words into holes, tell me what stops, physically, and the holes, and his voice coming back.`
- **Issue:** The reference prose renders the remembered phrase "tell me what stops, physically" in italics, indicating a different vocal quality (memory, distance). The narration script omits the italics, losing this performance cue.
- **Suggestion:** While tags are the primary tool, the performance should reflect this distinction. Add a tag like `[distant]` or `[remembered]` just before the phrase to cue the narrator. E.g., `...dropping his words into holes, [remembered] tell me what stops, physically, and the holes...`
- **Severity:** Medium

## Low Severity

- **Category:** Direction Density
- **Location:** `He crouched in front of the old server without being asked...`
- **Issue:** This long paragraph is governed by a single `[weary]` tag at the top of the scene. The paragraph moves from physical description of Eli's actions, to Lena's internal feeling of relief and mistrust, to her specific knowledge of his history with Asterion. A single tag doesn't capture these internal shifts.
- **Suggestion:** Add a tag to mark the turn in her thoughts. After "...fluent in a language she did not speak," insert a tag like `[weighing]` or `[guarded]` before "and she felt the old pull..." to color the complex mix of relief and mistrust.
- **Severity:** Low

- **Category:** Pacing & Peaks
- **Location:** `It was warm, the particular living warmth of a thing doing work, and under her palm the machine cycled, a small even push and release...`
- **Issue:** This is the first of the two key "bedside" passages that bookend the chapter. The script marks it `[controlled]`, which is good, but it could be given more deliberate weight to establish its importance, as called for in the pacing direction.
- **Suggestion:** Add a pacing tag like `[slowly]` or `[deliberately]` before the `[controlled]` tag to signal a more measured read for this specific sentence. E.g., `[slowly] [controlled] It was warm...`
- **Severity:** Low

- **Category:** Tag Craft
- **Location:** `[spare] "One scanner. One med unit. One controller."`
- **Issue:** The tag `[spare]` is accurate for Eli's overall register, but this specific line is an act of inventory or confirmation, fixing the count in his mind. The performance could be more precise.
- **Suggestion:** Use a more specific action-oriented tag for this line, such as `[confirming]` or `[reciting]`, to better capture the act of "fixing the count in place."
- **Severity:** Low

- **Category:** Tone Fit
- **Location:** `[tense] That was the worst of it, standing there.`
- **Issue:** The tag `[tense]` is a good choice to mark the escalation of her anger. However, the core of this paragraph is the cold clarity of her realization. "Tense" can sometimes imply agitation, which would be out of character.
- **Suggestion:** Consider a tag that emphasizes the coldness and clarity of the anger, such as `[hard]` or `[brittle]`, to ensure the performance stays grounded and avoids any hint of melodrama.
- **Severity:** Low

## Overall

This is an excellent, professional narration script. Its word-for-word fidelity is perfect, and the high-level voice direction is insightful and precise. The pacing is well-considered, and the major emotional peaks are handled with appropriate, austere weight. My suggestions are minor refinements aimed at bringing the granular, line-level tagging into even fuller alignment with the script's own brilliant direction—chiefly by adding more specific vocal texture for the patients and for Lena's moments of warmth, ensuring the performed registers are as distinct as the notes describe them. The foundation is exceptionally strong.
