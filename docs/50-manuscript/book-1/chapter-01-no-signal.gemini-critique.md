# Gemini Editorial Critique

## High Severity

-   **category**: continuity
-   **location**: Chapter Ending
-   **issue**: The chapter's ending correctly follows the detailed blueprint, closing on the clinic's midnight deadline. However, the high-level plot map (`docs/30-plot/book-1/chapters/chapter-01.md`) states the ending hook is the power-tier downgrade. This creates a contradiction between planning documents. The blueprint's ending is far stronger and the chapter is right to use it, but this inconsistency in the project's own authorities is a high-risk issue for future work.
-   **suggestion**: Reconcile the high-level plot map to match the superior structure of the chapter blueprint, making the clinic deadline the official ending hook for Chapter 1 project-wide.
-   **severity**: high

## Medium Severity

-   **category**: style
-   **location**: Multiple instances ("he was careful not to notice too closely", "He was good at keeping things separate", "he could feel himself reaching for the bench")
-   **issue**: The prose occasionally uses filtering language ("he felt," "he saw") or tells the reader about Eli's internal state directly, which violates the house style's preference for close-third person and showing over telling. This distances the reader from Eli's direct experience.
-   **suggestion**: Revise these instances to present the information as direct perception or action. For example, instead of "he was careful not to notice," show him deliberately looking away or focusing on a different detail. Instead of "he could feel himself reaching," simply state "he reached."
-   **severity**: medium

## Low Severity

-   **category**: prose
-   **location**: "He had lived through enough of these mornings not to feel anything sharper than a low, familiar irritation..."
-   **issue**: This is a minor instance of telling the reader Eli's emotional state directly at the chapter's opening, rather than showing it through his actions or more grounded sensory details.
-   **suggestion**: Consider rephrasing to show the habituated response, e.g., "It was the low, familiar irritation of a tool that had failed before he'd had coffee," grounding the feeling in the external world.
-   **severity**: low

-   **category**: dialogue
-   **location**: "Where'd you learn it? You don't pick that up at one of the programs. That's somewhere real. A company. Somewhere with money."
-   **issue**: The phrasing "That's somewhere real" is slightly clunky and less natural than the rest of the dialogue in the chapter.
-   **suggestion**: Consider a small tweak for flow, such as "That's from a real company. Somewhere with money."
-   **severity**: low

-   **category**: prose
-   **location**: "...a sixty-one-year-old man with a name that Lena was keeping."
-   **issue**: The phrasing "a name that Lena was keeping" is slightly awkward and indirect at the very end of a powerful paragraph.
-   **suggestion**: Sharpen the phrasing for a stronger final beat, e.g., "a name Lena hadn't shared" or "a name Lena was protecting."
-   **severity**: low

-   **category**: reveal-safety
-   **location**: "The rest of him went somewhere for half a second, a room with too much light in it, a voice that was not in this room saying his name with approval..."
-   **issue**: This is not a violation, but a note of caution. The hint at Eli's past is handled perfectly here, giving the shape of a memory without its contents. It is vital that this restraint is maintained and no more specific detail about Asterion or Mosaic is added.
-   **suggestion**: No change needed. This is a confirmation that the execution correctly navigates a high-risk area for reveal-safety.
-   **severity**: low

## Overall

This is an exceptionally strong chapter that executes its blueprint with precision and confidence. The sense of "administrative horror" and "managed decline" is established immediately and effectively. The escalation from a personal inconvenience (no signal) to a systemic, lethal threat (the clinic deadline) is masterfully paced. The character voices, particularly Lena's, are distinct and align perfectly with the style guide. The prose is clean and controlled, with several powerful lines ("you could not solder a decision," "less repair than amputation"). The few suggestions are minor refinements to prose and style rather than corrections to any structural or canonical weakness. The chapter successfully launches the novel's central premise and primary conflict.
