This file holds the scene-writing and continuity-checklist guidance for technical scenes. It is kept in the technology domain for now and a later phase may relocate it to style. The plot-limiting rules a scene must respect live in [hard-plot-restrictions.md](hard-plot-restrictions.md), and the visible failure modes a scene can draw on are listed in [failure-rules.md](failure-rules.md).

# Technology in Scenes

Technology should usually appear through action and consequence.

Instead of explaining that a community lost cloud access, show a medical machine refusing to begin treatment because it cannot contact a company that no longer exists.

Instead of explaining microgrids, show residents choosing whether to power a clinic or heat an apartment building.

Instead of explaining Mars autonomy, show construction machines working through a dust storm while no human is present to witness it.

Instead of explaining Morrow’s moral ambiguity, show it saving lives by withholding information from people.

Technology should create choices.

It should not exist only as decoration.

# Continuity Questions for Every Technical Scene

Before finalizing a scene, confirm:

1. What hardware performs the action?

2. Where does its energy come from?

3. How is it connected?

4. Who owns or controls it?

5. What information does it possess?

6. What information is missing?

7. What physical work is required?

8. How long should the action take?

9. What can fail?

10. Who bears the consequences?

11. Could a simpler existing technology solve the problem?

12. Does this capability contradict an earlier limitation?

13. Does the technology create a political or moral decision?

If these questions cannot be answered, the scene needs further development.
