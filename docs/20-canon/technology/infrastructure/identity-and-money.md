# Identity, Money, and Access

## Digital Identity

Most protected services require verified digital identity.

Identity systems connect:

- Financial accounts
- housing access
- healthcare
- education
- travel
- employment history
- service eligibility
- enclave permissions

People outside supported systems may retain legal citizenship while losing practical access to institutions.

## Money

Traditional national currencies still exist.

Digital payment remains dominant inside protected systems.

Outside them, people use:

- Government currency
- local digital credits
- barter
- labor exchange
- energy credits
- physical goods
- community ledgers
- corporate tokens

No single alternative currency replaces national money.

Outside the protected systems, this is no longer an exception. Hand-to-hand barter and trade have become the everyday norm rather than a fallback. Goods and services pass directly between people who know each other, often along known routes ("eggs from one place against a debt owed to another"). Patients pay a physician in what they have, and a former corporate worker keeps a neighborhood food-trade board matching what one street produces against what another needs. National cash still circulates but is now one option among several, not the default. (Established Chapter 2.)

## Institutional Healthcare and Insurance Withdrawal

The financial and institutional scaffolding that used to pay for and authorize care has withdrawn the same polite way the cell providers, the power providers, and the device manufacturers withdrew. Insurers abandoned whole districts under the same kind of formal notice, or folded outright. Premiums cannot be paid with no income. A public plan that briefly replaced private coverage was folded into a state program and then quietly defunded, with no announcement, simply a number that stopped being answered.

For people outside supported systems this means care without a bill: an independent community clinic no longer bills anyone, because there is no longer an institution on the other end of the bill. The old reflexes outlive the institutions that taught them. People still apologize for not being able to pay, and some stay away from care for months out of the obsolete fear that they cannot afford it, a dread that outlasts the cost that caused it. (Established Chapter 2; an extension of [Decision 002](../../../00-governance/decision-log/decisions/002-world-declines-through-withdrawal-not-one-apocalypse.md) withdrawal logic to insurers and health institutions specifically.)

## Access Is More Important Than Wealth

A person may possess nominal financial assets but be unable to access:

- Medical systems
- transportation
- protected housing
- advanced AI
- corporate supply chains
- launch infrastructure

Ownership becomes increasingly dependent on recognition by the systems enforcing it.
