# Identity, Money, and Access

## Digital Identity

Most protected services require verified digital identity.

Identity systems connect:

- Financial accounts
- housing access
- healthcare
- education
- travel
- employment history
- service eligibility
- enclave permissions

People outside supported systems may retain legal citizenship while losing practical access to institutions.

## Money

Traditional national currencies still exist.

Digital payment remains dominant inside protected systems.

Outside them, people use:

- Government currency
- local digital credits
- barter
- labor exchange
- energy credits
- physical goods
- community ledgers
- corporate tokens

No single alternative currency replaces national money.

## Access Is More Important Than Wealth

A person may possess nominal financial assets but be unable to access:

- Medical systems
- transportation
- protected housing
- advanced AI
- corporate supply chains
- launch infrastructure

Ownership becomes increasingly dependent on recognition by the systems enforcing it.
