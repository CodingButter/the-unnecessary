# Communications

## The Public Internet

The global internet still exists.

It is fragmented by service quality, ownership, and access.

Protected regions experience fast and reliable connectivity.

Unsupported regions experience:

- Intermittent access
- Bandwidth limits
- Unreliable routing
- Authentication failures
- Missing cloud services
- Regional shutdowns
- High latency
- Corporate blocking

## Local Networks

Communities build local networks using:

- Abandoned fiber
- Wi-Fi relays
- Mesh radios
- cellular equipment
- rooftop links
- repurposed satellite terminals
- wired neighborhood connections

These networks can support local communication even without the global internet.

## Cellular Networks

Many cellular towers remain physically intact.

A tower requires:

- Power
- Backhaul connectivity
- spectrum authorization
- control software
- maintenance
- compatible subscriber systems

Communities can repurpose towers for local service, but national roaming and commercial identity systems may not work.

## Satellites

Satellite communication remains available but controlled.

Asterion and other companies can restrict access through:

- Authentication
- pricing
- geographic limits
- bandwidth allocation
- terminal certification

Illegal or modified terminals exist.

They are detectable when transmitting.

## Communication Delays With Mars

Earth and Mars communications experience unavoidable delays based on orbital distance.

One-way delays vary from several minutes to more than twenty minutes.

There is no real-time conversation between Earth and Mars.

Martian systems must operate autonomously.

This is one reason the Aurelia Initiative depends heavily on artificial intelligence.
