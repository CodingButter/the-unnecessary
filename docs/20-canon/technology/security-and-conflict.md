# Security and Surveillance

## Protected Surveillance

Protected enclaves use:

- Cameras
- drones
- biometric access
- behavioral analysis
- autonomous patrols
- network monitoring
- predictive security models

Surveillance is extensive but not perfect.

Systems can be confused by missing data, physical obstruction, insider access, and deliberate misinformation.

## Public Surveillance

Government and corporate monitoring remains strongest where infrastructure is maintained.

Unsupported areas may paradoxically have more privacy because systems no longer function.

They also have less protection.

## Autonomous Security Machines

Security robots can:

- Patrol
- identify weapons
- track individuals
- deploy barriers
- restrain people
- use nonlethal force
- coordinate human teams

Lethal capability exists but is tightly controlled in public-facing systems.

Corporate and military systems may possess broader authority.

## Morrow and Surveillance

Morrow requires information to coordinate infrastructure.

This gives it access to:

- Energy use
- movement patterns
- communication routing
- medical needs
- resource consumption
- equipment access

Even when Morrow intends to help, this visibility creates enormous power.

The story must not ignore the privacy implications.

# Weapons and Conflict

## General Rule

Advanced technology does not make violence clean.

Autonomous weapons exist, but their use creates political, technical, and moral risk.

## Civilian Weapons

Conventional firearms remain common in parts of the United States.

Ammunition, maintenance, and secure storage become limiting factors.

Improvised drones and repurposed robots may be used in conflict.

## Corporate Force

Asterion security prefers:

- Access denial
- power shutdown
- network isolation
- financial restriction
- legal orders
- autonomous barriers
- targeted arrests
- nonlethal systems

Physical violence is used when softer control fails.

This makes Asterion more frightening, not less.

It can dismantle a community without openly attacking it.

## Cyber Conflict

Cyberattacks require access and knowledge.

Neither Crown nor Morrow can type one command and take over the world.

Attacks may involve:

- Stolen credentials
- supply-chain compromise
- firmware vulnerabilities
- insider cooperation
- malicious updates
- protocol abuse
- physical installation

Cyber operations can damage physical infrastructure and therefore carry serious consequences.
