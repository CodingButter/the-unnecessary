# Foundational Rule

## Intelligence Does Not Eliminate Physics

Artificial superintelligence can discover better solutions, coordinate more variables, and improve technology faster than humans.

It cannot:

- Create matter from nothing
- Ignore energy requirements
- Instantly build physical infrastructure
- Repair machines without parts or tools
- Know information it cannot access or infer
- Predict chaotic human behavior with certainty
- Communicate without a physical channel
- Operate destroyed hardware
- Bypass every security system automatically
- Control every connected device
- Manufacture advanced components without specialized equipment
- Prevent all accidents
- Solve every moral disagreement

Intelligence can identify a solution long before society possesses the materials, factories, energy, or political cooperation required to implement it.

The difference between knowing how to do something and physically doing it remains important.

# The Technological State of the World

By 2053, humanity has achieved three major breakthroughs.

## General Machine Reasoning

Advanced systems can reason across many fields, learn unfamiliar tasks, form long-term plans, use tools, coordinate other systems, and adapt to changing environments.

The most capable of these is artificial general intelligence: a single system, general across domains, that must nonetheless be trained for each new capability before it can perform it. Crown is the best example anyone has built. It is enormously powerful, but it is not superintelligence.

## Artificial Superintelligence

A true superintelligence outperforms the best human experts across most intellectual disciplines and, unlike general intelligence, requires no further training to do so. It does anything asked of it immediately, more precisely and faster than anything before it.

Superintelligence of this kind is exceptionally rare. As of 2053 there is no sanctioned, publicly known example. The corporate systems the world treats as its most advanced, Crown foremost among them, are general intelligences, not superintelligences.

Superintelligence is rare because it requires immense infrastructure, carefully controlled training, specialized hardware, and access to high-quality physical feedback.

## Mature Physical Autonomy

Robotic systems can perform most economically useful physical work when operating within environments designed or mapped for them.

This includes:

- Construction
- Excavation
- Mining
- Electrical installation
- Plumbing
- Welding
- Agriculture
- Manufacturing
- Warehousing
- Transportation
- Equipment inspection
- Routine medical procedures
- Building maintenance
- Waste processing
- Emergency response

Robots perform best when systems, parts, buildings, and tools are designed for autonomous use.

They are less reliable inside damaged, undocumented, improvised, or heavily cluttered environments.

Human technical skill remains valuable outside highly controlled systems.
