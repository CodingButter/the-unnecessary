# The Clinic Ward

"Six beds, four of them filled, the small machine-sounds and body-sounds of people sleeping or pretending to." The ward is "quiet in the way a ward is quiet, which is not silence."

## Layout and Dimensions

Six beds in a row. Lena starts where she always starts, the first bed, and moves the length of it under the work light.

## Occupants on the night of Chapter 2

- The Reyes man, with a leg dressed by hand with plain gauze and a neighbor's salve in place of the smart foam that stopped being supplied.
- Mrs. Diallo, with a cough that is "the kind of thing a chest holds for months," who stayed home too long for fear of a bill that no longer exists.
- Old Dembele, not a patient, in the spare bed where he comes some evenings because his own house has gone too cold, working his board-keeper's notebook of the food trade.

## Light and Atmosphere

Dim, the lights kept low, the cold in the floor; the small machine-sounds and the bodies. Warmth itself is the clinic's last comfort to give, "you're warm here, that's something."
