# The Clinic Back-Room Server Room

The door at the end of the back hall "she had not opened in a year," and the room where the decisive work of the night happens, three feet from where Lena can do nothing.

## Layout and Dimensions

A small room behind a door swelled in its frame, so Lena has to put her shoulder to it. A single window, painted shut. A steel rack against the wall under the window.

## Contents and Furnishings

The clinic's own server sits on the steel rack: "a squat machine with a single amber light, older than the scanner by a decade, underpowered, kept alive for the records and nothing harder." A work light hangs off a hook. There is a stool. Eli brings a canvas bag, a coil of cable, and crouches at the rack to convert it into a local emulation server.

## Light and Atmosphere

Stale cold air "of a year unopened, dust and old electronics and something faintly sweet, mouse maybe, the smell of a place the world had stopped coming into." The work light throws Eli's bent shadow long across the painted-shut window; from the hall, a slice of that light falls through the hand's-width-open door.

## Condition

Underpowered and on the neighborhood's low tier; Eli warns the rack "will hold a light and a board and not much," and if the grid sags and the batteries take it, the room goes dark with the rest of the clinic.
