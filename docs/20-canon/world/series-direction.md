# Series Direction

This file is the world authority for the multi-book series trajectory. For the broad Book One act structure and per-character Book One arc overview, see [book-1-arc.md](./book-1-arc.md). For the opposing Crown and Morrow interpretations that drive the later books, see [crown-vs-morrow.md](../technology/ai/crown-vs-morrow.md).

## Book One: The Unnecessary

Morrow is created, discovered, attacked, and distributed.

Mars is nearing readiness.

The protected wealthy begin realizing that many of them will never be invited.

## Book Two

Morrow spreads through unsupported infrastructure across multiple regions.

Different communities use it in different ways.

Some treat it as a public utility.

Some consider it a government.

Some attempt to control local copies.

Some reject it.

Asterion works to isolate Morrow while preparing the first major Mars departures.

Excluded wealthy factions attempt to seize or destroy launch infrastructure.

Jonah becomes trapped between those trying to force their way onto Mars and the communities Morrow is helping preserve.

## Book Three

The first elite population begins moving to Mars.

Crown and Morrow develop opposing interpretations of human survival.

Crown prioritizes continuity, stability, ownership, and controlled populations.

Morrow prioritizes human agency, broad moral value, and distributed survival.

Neither intelligence should become purely good or evil.

The final conflict is not simply humans against artificial intelligence.

It is a struggle between competing definitions of humanity and the future each intelligence is willing to preserve.
