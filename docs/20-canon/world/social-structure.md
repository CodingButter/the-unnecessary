# The Shape of Society

There are no universally accepted names for the social groups.

The world has not reorganized itself into three clearly labeled castes.

For planning purposes, the population can be understood through three broad conditions.

These labels should rarely appear in the actual narrative.

### The Gatekeepers

The highest level consists of the people who control the systems required to construct and operate the autonomous future.

They include founders of major artificial intelligence companies, space transportation companies, robotics manufacturers, energy networks, automated mining systems, advanced manufacturing firms, and private infrastructure providers.

They also include a small number of political partners, major shareholders, military allies, and family members.

Their importance does not come from income alone.

It comes from ownership and control.

They control launch systems.

They control the machines constructing Mars.

They control the intelligence coordinating those machines.

They control the private energy systems sustaining elite enclaves on Earth.

They decide which contracts continue, which communities receive support, and which individuals are allowed into their future.

Some are public celebrities.

Others have almost no public identity.

They are not united by a formal government.

They cooperate because their companies and personal ambitions depend upon one another.

They also distrust each other.

### The Protected Wealthy

Beneath the Gatekeepers are wealthy families, executives, investors, political intermediaries, senior professionals, early adopters, celebrities, and former public officials.

They possess enough wealth to avoid the immediate consequences of mass displacement.

They live in protected enclaves across the world.

Some enclaves are fortified suburbs.

Some are private sections of existing cities.

Some are islands, corporate campuses, resort towns, mountain communities, or converted military installations.

They have private power systems, dependable communications, maintained roads, advanced healthcare, food delivery, autonomous security, and access to premium artificial intelligence.

Their lives still resemble the comfort promised by the old world.

However, they do not control the systems supporting them.

Their security depends upon contracts, relationships, ownership stakes, and the continued interest of the Gatekeepers.

Most believe they will eventually be invited to Mars.

Very few possess an enforceable guarantee.

Their greatest fear is not the suffering beyond their walls.

Their greatest fear is discovering that they belong outside them.

### Everyone Else

The majority of humanity receives no shared name.

They still identify themselves by nation, city, neighborhood, culture, family, religion, occupation, or community.

Some journalists refer to them as the left behind.

Government agencies use phrases such as low-service regions, non-priority districts, unsupported municipalities, or economically inactive zones.

None of these labels becomes universal.

These people live inside an increasingly degraded version of the world they already knew.

They still occupy normal houses, apartments, suburbs, and cities.

They still drive aging cars.

They still use old smartphones.

They still watch television.

They still visit schools, stores, churches, clinics, and municipal buildings.

But the systems supporting those places become less reliable each year.

Their civilization is not replaced by a visibly new one.

It is frozen and slowly eroded.
