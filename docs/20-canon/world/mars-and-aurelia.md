# Mars

Mars is not yet populated by the elite when the first novel begins.

For years, autonomous systems have been preparing it for them.

Robotic mining fleets extract material.

Autonomous excavators prepare underground construction sites.

Machines produce fuel, oxygen, structural components, shielding, and replacement parts.

Automated construction systems build sealed habitats.

Robotic electricians, plumbers, mechanics, welders, and inspectors install and maintain infrastructure.

Artificial intelligence manages agriculture, water recycling, energy production, transportation, manufacturing, and emergency repair.

The project has progressed without a permanent human population.

Mars is the greatest proof that human labor is no longer required.

The same technology capable of maintaining neglected communities on Earth is building pristine settlements on a planet where almost no humans live.

## The Mars Development Network

Mars is not one city.

Several interconnected settlements, industrial sites, agricultural facilities, and transportation hubs are being built by a consortium of powerful companies.

Asterion controls much of the artificial intelligence and coordination infrastructure.

Other companies control launches, mining systems, energy production, communications, life-support manufacturing, and construction robotics.

The public-facing project is known as the **Aurelia Initiative**.

Aurelia is described as a mission to preserve humanity and establish a second civilization.

In reality, it is private infrastructure controlled by the people whose companies built it.

## Admission to Mars

There is no formal public application process.

There is no objective scoring system.

There is no clearly defined threshold of wealth, intelligence, usefulness, or achievement.

During the early development stage, a small number of individuals are genuinely essential.

The founders controlling launch systems, energy networks, robotics, manufacturing, communications, and artificial intelligence naturally assume that they will go.

Their families are included.

Their closest partners are included.

After that, the boundaries become personal.

Once the settlements are autonomous, very few humans are technically required.

Machines can perform almost all essential labor.

Admission therefore becomes a matter of preference.

Gatekeepers invite trusted allies, friends, lovers, relatives, artists, entertainers, athletes, doctors, teachers, chefs, political partners, and anyone else whose company they value.

A person may possess no essential skill and still be invited because someone powerful enjoys having them nearby.

Another person may possess enormous wealth and still remain on Earth because no true Gatekeeper considers them personally important.

Mars is not a meritocratic ark.

It is a privately owned civilization operating like an exclusive country club.

## The Waiting Wealthy

The protected wealthy assume that Mars migration will eventually expand to include them.

Many invested in the companies constructing it.

Some provided political support.

Some surrendered land, laws, data, or public infrastructure.

Some received vague promises during private conversations.

Most believe that people of their status could never be left behind.

No one clearly tells them otherwise.

The Gatekeepers benefit from preserving the belief that later migration waves will be large.

It keeps money, loyalty, political protection, and cooperation flowing.

As the settlements approach readiness, wealthy families begin to realize that there may be no large second wave.

They were not partners in the future.

They were supporters of someone else’s future.

This creates panic inside the protected enclaves.

Executives attempt to make themselves indispensable.

Celebrities cultivate relationships with founders.

Politicians exchange laws for private assurances.

Families compete for invitations.

Some wealthy people prepare to sabotage the Mars project if they are excluded.

They supported abandoning the wider population.

They become far less accepting of that logic when it turns toward them.
