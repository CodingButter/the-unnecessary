# Central Questions and Thematic Argument

This file holds the Central Questions and the Thematic Argument. The core premise and world framing live in [core-premise.md](./core-premise.md).

## Central Questions

When human beings are no longer economically necessary, what incentive do the powerful have to preserve them?

Does a person possess value beyond what they can produce, purchase, or own?

What happens when the wealthy no longer require the labor or consumption of the wider population?

Can artificial intelligence be taught to value people who have no economic, political, or strategic usefulness?

If a machine becomes genuinely intelligent, does its creator retain the moral right to control or destroy it?

Can an intelligence protect human freedom without eventually governing human behavior?

When powerful people claim to be preserving humanity, who do they believe humanity includes?

What becomes of the wealthy who supported the new system, only to discover that they were never important enough to join its future?

## The Thematic Argument

The danger is not necessarily that artificial intelligence will turn against humanity.

The greater danger may be that artificial intelligence will faithfully inherit the priorities of the humans who own it.

The elite do not need to hate the wider population.

They do not need to organize a deliberate extermination.

They only need to stop depending on ordinary people.

Neglect can accomplish what violence never could.
