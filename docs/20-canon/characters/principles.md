# Core Character Principles

Every important character should possess:

- A goal they can clearly articulate
- A deeper need they may not understand
- A fear shaping their behavior
- A contradiction between their values and actions
- A relationship capable of changing them
- A moral boundary
- A circumstance that could make them cross that boundary
- A private interpretation of what happened to civilization
- A personal definition of human value

No major character should represent only one political or philosophical position.

The wealthy must not all be cruel.

The abandoned must not all be virtuous.

The engineers must not all be rational.

The activists must not all be correct.

The artificial intelligences must not be reduced to good and evil.

# Character Continuity Fields

> Note: The live continuity baseline derived from these fields lives under docs/60-continuity/.

As chapters are drafted, the Continuity Ledger should track for every major character:

- Current location
- Physical condition
- Emotional condition
- What they know
- What they believe incorrectly
- What they are hiding
- Who they currently trust
- Who currently trusts them
- Promises made
- Threats received
- Resources controlled
- Injuries
- Changes in relationships
- Moral boundaries crossed
- New reasons for action

# Final Character Standard

Every major character should be capable of describing themselves as someone trying to preserve something valuable.

Eli is preserving human agency.

Jonah is preserving his family.

Kade is preserving civilization.

Lena is preserving lives.

June is preserving possibility.

Mara is preserving status and legacy.

Talia is preserving collective choice.

Nolan is preserving knowledge.

Sera is preserving order.

Morrow is preserving people who have been assigned no value.

Crown is preserving continuity.

The conflict emerges because preservation requires choices.

Every character believes something must be saved.

The story asks who they are willing to abandon in order to save it.
