# Evan Voss

## Basic Information

**Full name:** Evan Whitmore Voss (the surname Voss and given name Evan are `[canon, voss-mara.md]`; the middle name Whitmore is accepted as canon (Decision 056) and does not encode the undefined father, who remains an open slot per the relational spine)
**Common name:** Evan [canon, voss-mara.md]
**Age at the start of Book One:** 27 [canon, character-birth-dates.md]
**Birth date:** November 4, 2025 [canon, character-birth-dates.md]
**Birthplace:** Lansing, Michigan
**Current residence:** Lakeward, the protected enclave north of Detroit, per `../../world/locations/greater-detroit/lakeward.md`. Evan lives carelessly inside Lakeward. [canon, voss-mara.md]
**Household:** Lives in his own residence within Lakeward, separate from Mara's household but funded through it; staffed and maintained by enclave service contracts and an autonomous household system rather than by Evan. He does not share a roof with his mother. [open]
**Occupation:** None in any earning sense. He has no profession, no trade, and no institutional role. His days are leisure, socializing, and consumption inside the enclave. [canon that he has no useful expertise, voss-mara.md]
**Faction or class:** Protected Wealthy, Lakeward, per `../../world/social-structure.md`. [open] He is the inherited, second-generation version of his class: protected by his mother's standing rather than by anything he built.
**Primary viewpoint:** No. Evan is never a point-of-view character. He is seen from the outside, chiefly through Mara.
**Story role:** The human stake under Mara Voss's arc. Evan personifies his mother's exact fear, that protected wealth becomes worthless the moment the Gatekeepers stop needing the bloodline. A twenty-seven-year-old heir with nothing a Gatekeeper wants is the next-generation proof of that fear, walking around the enclave, charming and oblivious. [open]

## Physical and Identifiers

![Portrait of Evan Voss](../portraits/voss-evan.jpg)

### Frame

Six feet even, with the easy, unearned good build of a man who has had the best food, the best sleep, and trainers his whole life and never once had to use the strength for anything. He is starting, just barely, to go soft at the waist and under the jaw, the first comfortable thickening of a body that has never been asked to be hard. His posture is loose and open, weight back on the heels, the carriage of someone who has never had to brace for a closing door or a cold room. He takes up space without effort and without apology.

### Coloring

Fair, lightly tanned in the even way of a man with leisure and good light, never weathered. Blond hair worn a touch long and expensively careless, thick, kept by a barber who comes to the house, the same blond Mara's has been going gray out of, so the family resemblance reads at a glance. Blue-gray eyes, clear and a little unfocused, the eyes of a person who has never had to watch anything carefully because nothing has ever depended on his watching.

**Heritage:** White American: the same West-Michigan Dutch, German, and Scandinavian line.

### Face

A pleasant, open, symmetrical face, the kind people decide to like before he has said anything. High color in the cheeks. A wide, ready, slightly lazy smile that he deploys as a first move and a fallback both. His resting expression is mild, unbothered, faintly amused, the look of a man waiting to be entertained. He has Mara's straight nose and good bones under softer flesh; you can see the formidable face his mother has and the comfortable one he has chosen to wear instead.

### Hands and handedness

Right-handed. Soft, broad, unmarked hands with clean buffed nails and not one callus, scar, or stain on them. They are the deliberate inverse of every working hand in this book: where Marisol's hands read cold and cash and Eli's read repair, Evan's hands read that he has never fixed, stocked, dressed, lifted, or maintained a single thing. His hands are the body of his uselessness. He gestures with them a great deal, easily and gracefully, and has never once needed them to keep anything running. [behavior-only] (proposed)

### Distinguishing marks

The telling thing is the absence. No scars, no burns, no work-marks, because nothing in his life has ever cut, burned, or worn him. Even, professionally maintained teeth, cosmetically corrected in adolescence, the dentistry of a class that still has dentists. A small, nearly invisible scar inside one elbow from the placement of a premium health-monitoring implant in his early twenties, the way a watch used to be a coming-of-age gift. A faint pale band on a tan wrist from a watch he wears for looks. No tattoos. No piercings. The unmarked body is itself the identifier.

### Identity and body status (2053)

Fully and premium-registered, the exact opposite of the stranded Everyone Else, per `../../technology/infrastructure/identity-and-money.md`. His verified digital identity is current, gold-tier, and opens every protected door without his ever thinking about it: enclave permissions, advanced AI access, premium healthcare, dependable communications, food delivery, autonomous transport. [open] He has casual luxury augmentations he barely registers as augmentations: the health-monitoring implant noted above, and a neural-linked personal assistant intelligence that runs his calendar, his payments, and his social logistics so he has never had to. He has no chronic conditions; his body has been monitored and optimized since birth. The thing to notice is that all of this is provided, not held. His access depends entirely on his mother's standing and the continued interest of the systems above her, exactly the dependency `../../world/social-structure.md` names as the protected wealthy's real condition. He does not know this the way Marisol knows her case is dying. [behavior-only] (proposed reading)

### Movement and voice

He moves with an unhurried, loose-limbed ease, never rushing, because he has never in his life had to. A relaxed, ambling gait; he leans on things, drapes over furniture, settles. His voice is a warm, fluent baritone with the placeless, polished non-accent of premium enclave media and private schooling, the accent engineered to come from nowhere and signal everywhere. The timbre is easy and unforced. He has never had to raise it to be heard, so under real pressure it thins and climbs rather than hardening, the voice of a man whose displeasure has always been enough.

### Grooming and default dress

Effortless, current, and expensive worn carelessly, the costume of inherited rather than performed status. Default dress: soft technical-luxury casuals, fine knitwear, good unbranded fabrics that cost more than a month of an outside household, premium trainers, a quiet good watch. He is never overdressed and never quite trying. Crucially, he is never cold and has never owned a coat for warmth's sake, only for style; he has never in his life worn a coat indoors, the single tell that most cleanly separates his world from Marisol's, who keeps her coat on inside because the shop is cold. [behavior-only] (proposed) Clean-shaven or a maintained light stubble. Scent of a good understated cologne, clean laundry, and the faint ozone of climate-perfect rooms.

## Personality

In public Evan is genuinely charming, warm, generous with his attention, and easy to be around. People like him quickly and he likes being liked; it is the closest thing he has to a vocation. He is not cruel and not stupid. He is unserious, incurious about anything that does not touch his own pleasure, and profoundly untested. In private, in the rare quiet, there is a thin restlessness under the ease, a boredom he keeps outrunning, and lately a low unfamiliar unease he does not have the equipment to name. [behavior-only] (proposed)

His humor is light, quick, teasing, and self-deprecating in a way that costs him nothing, because he has never been genuinely diminished. He is funny about small things and silent about large ones. He deflects weight with a joke and a change of subject, a reflex so smooth he does not notice he is doing it. [behavior-only] (proposed)

**Articulated goal:** To keep his life pleasant, full, and amusing, and to be the most relaxed and best-liked person in any room. He does not consciously frame Mars as a goal; he treats it as a fact already settled in his favor. [open]
**Deeper need:** To matter in his own right, as someone of value rather than as Mara Voss's funded son, though he has never let the thought fully surface. [behavior-only] (proposed)
**Governing fear:** Consciously, boredom and being ordinary among his peers. Underneath, and not yet looked at directly, the dread that he is not necessary to anyone with the power to keep him, that without the name and the money he is nothing. [behavior-only] (proposed)
**Core contradiction:** He believes he is free, independent, and his own man, while being completely dependent for everything on his mother's standing and the systems above her. [open]
**Moral boundary:** He will not be deliberately cruel to someone in front of him, and he will not knowingly do harm with his own hands. He is careless, not vicious.
**What could make them cross it:** Real fear of losing his place. If exclusion from Mars became concrete, Evan would, out of panic rather than malice, reach for whatever leverage was nearest, including trading on his mother's secret, telling himself it was survival and not betrayal. [reveal: Book 2] (proposed)
**Private reading of the collapse:** He barely has one. To Evan the withdrawal is abstract backdrop, something that happened to other people in places he does not go. The world sorted itself into an inside and an outside, he ended up inside, and that strikes him as natural rather than as anything that required a victim. His non-thinking about it is the point. [behavior-only] (proposed)
**Personal definition of human value:** Unexamined, but in practice: you are worth being fun, charming, tasteful, pleasant to have around. The cruel irony is that this is almost exactly the currency that actually buys a place on Mars, being personally enjoyable to a Gatekeeper, and Evan has spent the whole of it on his peers, on an audience that cannot admit him to anything. [behavior-only] (proposed reading against `../../world/mars-and-aurelia.md`)
**What they are preserving:** Ease. Lightness. The belief that life is supposed to be pleasant and that some people simply get to enjoy being alive. He is, without knowing it, the last unworried member of a class about to learn worry. (His entry in the Final Character Standard.) [open]

## Daily Life and Habits

Evan's daily life is the deliberate inverse of the everyday-economy days written for the Everyone Else cast. He produces nothing and trades nothing. He consumes. Where Marisol checks two dying dairy doors at dawn and Dembele keeps a cork food-board, Evan's day is leisure inside a system he never has to maintain, per `../../world/protected-enclaves.md`. [open]

He wakes late in a climate-perfect room. His neural-linked assistant has already arranged the day: a trainer, a court or the artificial lake, lunch somewhere good in the enclave, a social calendar of people exactly like him. For money he does not earn or barter; he draws on family accounts through his premium verified identity, the dominant frictionless digital payment of the protected systems, per `../../technology/infrastructure/identity-and-money.md`, and an allowance arrangement managed through Mara's people. He has never touched a community ledger, a barter trade, soft worn cash, or a food-board, and would not know how. [behavior-only] (proposed)

He eats whatever is delivered or served, beautifully and on time. He sleeps deeply and long. He does not commute so much as glide, by autonomous car within the enclave's maintained roads. He pays for nothing in any way he can feel, which is the whole problem and which he experiences as normal life. His evenings are parties, screens, and company. He almost never leaves Lakeward, and when the unprotected world comes up at all it is as rumor or backdrop, never destination. [open]

## Hobbies and Interests

- Sailing and racing small craft on Lakeward's artificial lake, the one physical skill he has actually acquired, because it is play and looks well and costs money rather than effort.
- A revolving series of half-started creative projects he never finishes: a personal music label, a curated supper club, a "brand," each abandoned the moment it threatens to become work. The unfinished projects are themselves a portrait of a man who has never had to complete anything.
- Connoisseurship as a pastime: wine, food, design, immersive entertainment, the accumulated taste of a class with leisure, which he genuinely enjoys and is genuinely good at, and which is the one currency he has mistaken for security.

## Likes and Dislikes

Likes: warmth and warm rooms, which he has never had to do without; food arriving without being asked for; being the most relaxed person present; his own laugh in a good room; the artificial lake at evening; his mother's competence, which he leans on without seeing; being told yes. Dislikes: friction of any kind; being told no, which barely registers as real information; boredom; the cold; the abstract, distasteful idea of the outside; and, lately and uneasily, his mother's new tension, the worry she has started carrying that he can feel without understanding. [behavior-only on the last item]

**Adrian Kade** (`./kade-adrian.md`), the Gatekeeper Evan assumes will admit him, and with whom he has no actual relationship. **Lakeward peer circle**. A loose set of fellow enclave inheritors his own age, the audience for his charm and the holders of his social debts. They are the wrong audience: liking him buys him nothing, because none of them can admit anyone to anything. What he wants from them is to be the best-liked of the set; what they reflect back is exactly his miscalculation about where value is banked.

## Voice and Speech

Warm, fluent, breezy, and unhurried. He speaks in easy, confident, slightly lazy sentences, full of casual superlatives ("amazing," "the best," "no, genuinely the best"), and he charms rather than argues. He is never urgent because nothing has ever been urgent. He tells small jokes, gives compliments that cost him nothing, and changes the subject smoothly the instant a conversation turns heavy. [behavior-only] (proposed) Where his mother says "the situation requires" and "there must be a mechanism," Evan says "it'll be fine," "it's handled," "don't worry about it," and means it, because it always has been. Under real pressure his ease cracks into petulant disbelief; a genuine "no" he hears as a mistake to be corrected or an opener to be negotiated, never as a fact, and his voice thins and climbs rather than steadying. [behavior-only] (proposed)

## History and Background

Born November 4, 2025 `[canon]` in Lansing, Michigan, to Mara Voss as she was rising through Michigan state politics during the infrastructure crisis, per `./voss-mara.md`. His parents divorced; the father is unnamed and not invented here. [canon that Mara is divorced, voss-mara.md] (the timing in his early childhood is proposed in Section 10)

Evan grew up as the politician's, and later the Governor's, son, raised inside the protective bubble by staff and systems while Mara did the public work of negotiating the corporate takeovers that preserved some services and accelerated the abandonment of others. [canon that Mara was Governor and made those agreements, voss-mara.md] He was schooled in private enclave institutions, never wanted for anything, and crossed into adulthood inside Lakeward without ever building a profession, a skill the world needs, or a relationship with anyone who controls the future. By Book One he is twenty-seven, living carelessly in his own funded corner of Lakeward, certain that his family's standing guarantees a place on Mars. [canon, voss-mara.md]

The defining fact of his history is what is missing from it: he has never experienced the withdrawal. By the time the world outside began visibly to deteriorate, he was already inside the walls, and he has no memory and no model of a system failing. He is the protected generation in its purest form, the demand-side proof, in reverse, of the world `../../world/social-structure.md` describes. [open]

## Secrets

- He has started, in a way he refuses to look at directly, to sense that he has no idea how the family's security actually works or who guarantees it, and that he may have nothing any Gatekeeper wants. He hides the dawning dread under more socializing. Exposure, even to himself, would collapse the ease that is his whole identity. [reveal: Book 1] (proposed)
- He has been quietly living and spending against an assumed inheritance and an assumed Mars place, accruing social and financial debts among his circle on the security of a future no one has actually confirmed to him. [reveal: Book 1] (proposed)
- If Mars exclusion became real, he would be the first, out of panic rather than malice, to try to trade on whatever leverage his mother holds, including her archived documents, telling himself it was survival. This makes him a live hazard to Mara's own secret and a possible trigger for her arc. [reveal: Book 2] (proposed; ties to the secret in `./voss-mara.md`)

## Role and Series Potential

In Book One Evan's function is precise and mostly passive: he is the human stake under Mara's arc, the embodiment of the thing she fears, charming and oblivious in the foreground while her certainty curdles into fear behind him. His obliviousness should sharpen, not soften, as her fear grows; the gap between what he assumes and what is true is the engine. His Book One arc is shallow and downward into the first dim awareness that the future is not, in fact, handled. [open]

Long-term series potential: Evan is a fork. He could be the trigger that forces Mara's hand, his concrete exclusion or his panicked reach for her leverage detonating her arc. Or he could be the rarer thing, the protected-wealth child who is forced, for the first time, to become genuinely useful, a slow and humbling competence arc that mirrors in one generation down what his mother must also learn. False belief: that he is owed the future because of who his mother is. Truth he must learn: the future is bought with leverage and with personal value to the powerful, and a name purchases nothing once it stops being needed, which is his mother's truth, inherited and one generation later. [open]

Writing rules: do not make Evan a cartoon of the entitled rich; his charm and his decency-in-the-small are real, which is what makes his uselessness tragic rather than contemptible. Do not let him understand his own situation faster than Mara understands hers. Keep him likeable. Never let him voice the theme; his not-understanding is the device. His hands, his never having been cold, and his inability to hear "no" are the three physical tells; use them rather than explanation.
