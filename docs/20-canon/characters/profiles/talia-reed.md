# Talia Reed

## Basic Information

**Full name:** Talia Simone Reed
**Age:** 35
**Birthplace:** Detroit, Michigan
**Current residence:** Eli’s neighborhood
**Former occupation:** Public-school science teacher
**Current occupation:** Community coordinator and local communications organizer
**Primary viewpoint:** Possible occasional viewpoint
**Story role:** Political representative of the community and human counterweight to technical authority

## Physical Appearance

Talia is five feet eight inches tall with an athletic build.

She wears her hair in long braids usually tied back.

She dresses simply and always carries an old tablet containing local records, agreements, supply counts, and contact lists.

Her expression is open until she senses evasion.

## Background

Talia taught middle-school science before local schools lost stable funding.

She remained after many professional workers relocated.

She organized food distribution, emergency classes, medical transport, and neighborhood communications.

She is not an engineer, but she understands people, institutions, and collective decision-making better than Eli does.

## Personality

Talia is practical, emotionally intelligent, skeptical, and persistent.

She dislikes both corporate authority and romantic ideas about spontaneous community.

She knows people cooperate when structures make cooperation possible.

She is willing to use rules, meetings, persuasion, shame, and compromise.

## Relationship With Eli

Talia respects Eli’s ability but resents his habit of making urgent technical decisions without community consultation.

She believes Eli hides political choices inside engineering language.

Eli believes Talia sometimes prioritizes process when delay could cause physical harm.

Their conflict should remain serious and productive.

There is potential for emotional closeness, but no immediate romance.

## External Goal

Build a durable form of local governance capable of surviving infrastructure withdrawal.

## Internal Need

Accept that not every emergency can wait for consensus.

## Greatest Fear

Talia fears replacing corporate abandonment with dependence on one brilliant engineer or one powerful intelligence.

## Core Contradiction

She believes in collective consent but occasionally manipulates meetings and information to achieve outcomes she considers necessary.

## Moral Boundary

Talia will not allow Morrow or Eli to become the unaccountable government of the neighborhood.

## What Could Make Her Cross It

If democratic disagreement threatens immediate survival, she may accept emergency authority and struggle to surrender it later.

## Secret

Talia has privately prepared plans to isolate or destroy parts of Morrow if it begins controlling community systems without consent.

## False Belief

A legitimate process can contain any form of power.

## Truth She Must Learn

Some forms of power can move faster than institutions designed to govern them.

## Book One Arc

Talia supports Morrow’s practical benefits while demanding oversight.

She becomes a central organizer during the containment crisis.

She may be the person who persuades residents not to surrender Morrow immediately while also preventing Eli from making the decision alone.

## Speech Pattern

Talia speaks plainly and asks who benefits, who decides, and who bears the cost.

She uses classroom habits when explaining complex issues.

She dislikes jargon and forces experts to translate.
