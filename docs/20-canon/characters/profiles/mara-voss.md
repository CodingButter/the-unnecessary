# Mara Voss

## Basic Information

**Full name:** Mara Elise Voss
**Age:** 56
**Birthplace:** Grand Rapids, Michigan
**Current residence:** Lakeward
**Former occupation:** Governor of Michigan
**Current occupation:** Strategic adviser, investor, and political broker
**Primary viewpoint:** Occasional
**Story role:** Representative of protected wealth discovering its own disposability

## Physical Appearance

Mara is tall, elegant, and carefully composed.

She has blonde hair beginning to gray and refuses aggressive age-reversal treatments because she believes visible age communicates authority.

Her clothing is formal even in private environments.

She controls her expressions in public and allows anger to appear only through stillness.

## Political History

Mara rose through state politics during the infrastructure crisis.

She negotiated agreements allowing Asterion and other corporations to assume control over power, transportation, and security systems.

She argued that private systems were the only realistic way to prevent total collapse.

Her decisions preserved some services.

They also accelerated the abandonment of unprofitable communities.

Mara considers herself one of the people who prevented Michigan from becoming uninhabitable.

Many people outside protected districts consider her a collaborator.

Both interpretations contain truth.

## Relationship With Asterion

Mara helped create the legal structure protecting Asterion’s autonomous facilities and Mars assets.

She believed this made her a founding political partner in Aurelia.

Kade never explicitly promised her passage.

He allowed her to assume it.

## Family

Mara is divorced.

She has one adult son, **Evan Voss**, age twenty-seven, who lives carelessly inside Lakeward and assumes Mars access is guaranteed.

Mara’s fear intensifies when she realizes Evan possesses neither useful expertise nor a personal relationship with a Gatekeeper.

## Personality

Mara is strategic, disciplined, intelligent, proud, and deeply sensitive to humiliation.

She is capable of empathy but tends to treat suffering as an issue of scale and policy.

She respects strength and despises visible desperation, including her own.

## External Goal

Secure Mars passage for herself and her son.

## Internal Need

Recognize that the system she helped build did not fail when it excluded her.

It operated according to the same principles she supported.

## Greatest Fear

Mara fears becoming irrelevant while still alive to witness it.

## Core Contradiction

She believes ownership and contribution should determine access until personal relationships are used to exclude her.

Then she demands fairness.

## Moral Boundary

Mara will not intentionally cause mass civilian casualties.

## What Could Make Her Cross It

If sabotaging part of the Mars infrastructure appears to be the only way to force negotiations, she may accept predictable deaths as leverage.

## Secret

Mara possesses archived legal documents proving that Asterion deliberately encouraged wealthy investors to expect future Mars access without intending to accommodate them.

These documents could destabilize protected enclaves and threaten the Aurelia Initiative.

## False Belief

The powerful respect contribution.

## Truth She Must Learn

The powerful respect control and personal loyalty. Contribution without leverage is temporary.

## Book One Arc

Mara begins confident in her future.

She discovers that less influential people have received invitations because of personal relationships.

She quietly investigates.

Her fear turns into anger.

By the end of Book One, she is connected to a faction considering sabotage.

## Long-Term Arc

Mara may become an uneasy ally to Eli’s communities while remaining ideologically opposed to Morrow’s egalitarian principles.

She does not become a revolutionary.

She becomes a powerful person trying to survive exclusion.

## Speech Pattern

Mara speaks with political clarity.

She rarely says “I want.”

She says:

- “The situation requires.”
- “We need to establish.”
- “There must be a mechanism.”
- “This arrangement is not sustainable.”

When privately emotional, she becomes blunt and personal.

## Writing Rules

Do not redeem Mara too quickly.

Her suffering does not erase the suffering her policies caused.

She should remain formidable even when afraid.
