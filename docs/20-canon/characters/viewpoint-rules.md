# Viewpoint Guidance and Dialogue Differentiation

This file owns the rules for point-of-view allocation across Book One and for how each character is distinguished in dialogue. For per-character identity and arc, see the profiles under profiles/. For pairwise relationship dynamics, see relationship-map.md. For prose-level voice and style conventions beyond character dialogue, defer to the style guide at ../../10-vision/style/index.md.

# Viewpoint Guidance

## Primary Viewpoint

Eli should receive approximately half of Book One’s chapters.

## Secondary Viewpoints

Jonah and Kade should receive enough chapters to reveal worlds Eli cannot access.

Lena, June, and Mara should receive occasional viewpoint chapters.

Talia may receive one or two chapters if necessary.

Morrow and Crown should not receive direct internal viewpoint chapters during Book One.

Their interiority must remain uncertain.

## Viewpoint Rule

A viewpoint chapter should exist only when that character can reveal:

- Information unavailable to Eli
- A distinct emotional interpretation
- A meaningful decision
- A consequence Eli cannot witness

Do not change viewpoint merely to create artificial suspense.

# Dialogue Differentiation

## Eli

Precise, restrained, dry, literal under stress.

## Jonah

Smooth, relational, reassuring, evasive when afraid.

## Kade

Measured, philosophical, controlled, reframes assumptions.

## Lena

Direct, practical, consequence-focused.

## June

Fast, casual, technically playful, formal when frightened.

## Mara

Political, structured, authoritative, personal only in private.

## Talia

Plainspoken, process-focused, asks who decides and who pays.

## Nolan

Concrete, experienced, skeptical of theory.

## Sera

Operational, brief, controlled.

## Morrow

Precise, curious, increasingly interpretive.

## Crown

Adaptive, polished, context-sensitive.
