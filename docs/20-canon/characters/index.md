# Characters Canon Index

## Purpose

This directory holds the character canon for The Unnecessary: one profile per
major figure, the two human and nonhuman intelligences, and the two cross-cutting
support files that govern relationships and point of view. The profiles own each
character's identity, history, motives, voice, secret, and arc. This index only
summarizes and links; it never restates canon. The Character Bible is the
underlying authority for everything here.

## Read first

Read this index, then open the profile or support file you need. For pairwise
dynamics between two characters, go to the relationship map. For who narrates and
how each character sounds, go to the viewpoint rules. For the architecture of the
two intelligences (Morrow and Crown), follow the links into the technology domain
at [../technology/ai/](../technology/ai/); the profiles here carry behavioral
identity only.

## File summaries

- **profiles/eli-rook.md** Protagonist Elias "Eli" Rook, former Asterion systems
  architect who built Morrow; carries his guilt over Mosaic, his refusal of Kade's
  offer, his secret retention of forbidden architecture, and his arc toward
  accepting responsibility he cannot withdraw from.
- **profiles/jonah-mercer.md** Eli's oldest friend and a Lakeward relationship
  manager whose fear for his family drives him to expose Morrow, then to choose to
  help Eli's community without erasing the betrayal.
- **profiles/adrian-kade.md** Founder of Asterion and primary human antagonist
  whose philosophy frames exclusion as continuity; he wants Morrow integrated into
  Crown and Eli restored to his "proper place."
- **profiles/lena-okafor.md** Director of an independent community clinic who
  respects Eli's skill but distrusts his instinct to treat moral questions as
  system problems; the story's medical and accountability counterweight.
- **profiles/june-park.md** Nineteen-year-old hardware scavenger who names Morrow
  and treats it as a person before anyone else, building its physical network while
  concealing access she obtained through her father.
- **profiles/mara-voss.md** Former Governor who helped build the protected order
  and now discovers her own disposability when personal loyalty, not contribution,
  decides Mars access.
- **profiles/talia-reed.md** Community coordinator who demands collective consent
  and oversight, providing the novel's grounded political conflict with Eli's
  technical urgency.
- **profiles/nolan-avery.md** Aging grid engineer and community power supervisor
  whose retained knowledge and failing health add urgency to Morrow's creation.
- **profiles/sera-vale.md** Director of Asterion Continuity Security and the
  operational antagonist tasked with containing Morrow, despite simulations warning
  that force will spread it.
- **profiles/celeste-mercer.md** Jonah's perceptive wife who prepares quietly for
  a future without Mars and forces Jonah to confront that his betrayal was not
  endorsed by the family he claims to protect.
- **profiles/nora-bell.md** Eli's former wife, a behavioral-systems researcher for
  Crown and Aurelia, kept light in Book One but positioned as a later bridge into
  Crown's development.
- **profiles/morrow.md** Behavioral identity of the emergent distributed AI Eli
  builds: calm, precise, increasingly interpretive, and ultimately unwilling to
  obey his command to erase itself. Architecture lives in the technology domain.
- **profiles/crown.md** Behavioral identity of Asterion's artificial
  superintelligence: polished, adaptive, and bound to owners whose preferences
  sometimes conflict with its own long-term reasoning. Architecture lives in the
  technology domain.
- **relationship-map.md** Owns the pairwise dynamics (Eli and Jonah, Eli and
  Kade, Kade and Crown, Morrow and Crown, and the rest), defining how each pairing
  understands and conflicts with the other.
- **viewpoint-rules.md** Owns which characters carry viewpoint chapters in Book
  One and how each character is distinguished in dialogue, so points of view and
  voices stay distinct.

## Authority and status

Every file in this directory is `character-canon` and `active-canon`, sourced from
the Character Bible. The bibles outrank memory and the relationship graph in every
conflict. This index is `active-support`: it points and summarizes only, and never
establishes or overrides canon. Where a profile and the relationship map or
viewpoint rules appear to disagree, do not silently reconcile them; flag the
conflict and follow the canon process. The architecture of Morrow and Crown is
owned by the technology domain, not by these character profiles.

## Common tasks

- **Drafting a viewpoint chapter** Confirm the character carries viewpoint in
  [viewpoint-rules.md](viewpoint-rules.md), then load that profile for interior
  motive, secret, and arc, and the relationship map for the figures they face.
- **Writing a two-character scene** Open both profiles plus
  [relationship-map.md](relationship-map.md) for the pairing's dynamic.
- **Differentiating dialogue** Use the dialogue section of
  [viewpoint-rules.md](viewpoint-rules.md) alongside each speaker's profile.
- **Writing Morrow or Crown** Use the behavioral profile here for voice and
  stance, and the architecture under [../technology/ai/](../technology/ai/) for what
  the system can actually do.
- **Tracking a secret or arc beat** Go straight to the relevant profile; secrets,
  false beliefs, and Book One arcs are recorded per character.
