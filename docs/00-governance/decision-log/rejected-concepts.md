# Explicitly Rejected Concepts

The following ideas should not be reintroduced casually.

## Rejected Setting Concepts

- A district called the Stack
- Poor residents living primarily in stacked shipping containers
- A universal neon cyberpunk slum
- A completely ruined wasteland world
- One elite supercity containing all wealthy people
- Mars as one glass-domed city

## Rejected Social Concepts

- A universally named underclass
- A clean three-caste system
- A formal “Continuity Class”
- A universal “Unserved” identity
- Every wealthy person automatically receiving Mars access
- A unified elite with no internal conflict

## Rejected Mars Concepts

- A public merit score
- A contribution ranking
- A lottery
- A formal ark-selection board
- An objective passenger class
- A fully populated Mars before Book One
- Mars requiring large numbers of human workers after mature autonomy

## Rejected AI Concepts

- Morrow discovered fully formed
- Morrow as a secret Asterion AI
- Morrow as instantly omnipotent
- Morrow as perfectly benevolent
- Crown as secretly murderous from the beginning
- AI ignoring power, hardware, network, or manufacturing limits
- Direct Morrow viewpoint chapters in Book One

## Rejected Character Concepts

- Eli as an innocent outsider
- Jonah as a simple traitor
- Kade as a sadistic caricature
- Lena as always morally correct
- June as an infallible young genius
- Mara becoming heroic merely because she is excluded

## Rejected Plot Concepts

- A sudden machine apocalypse
- Immediate armed corporate invasion
- A clean revolution that quickly repairs society
- Morrow solving every infrastructure and political problem
- Destruction of one server permanently defeating a distributed intelligence
- A simple Earth-versus-Mars war
