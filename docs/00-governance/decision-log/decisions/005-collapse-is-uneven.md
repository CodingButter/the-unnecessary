## Decision 005: Collapse Is Uneven

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Worldbuilding

### Decision

Conditions differ dramatically between nearby communities.

One town may retain power because utility engineers live there.

Another may preserve communications through an improvised local network.

A third may lose both.

Knowledge, cooperation, physical infrastructure, and access to parts determine survival.

### Previous or Alternative Direction

A uniformly collapsed world was rejected.

### Reason

Real infrastructure decline is uneven.

This also creates more varied locations, conflicts, alliances, and moral choices.

### Consequences

- Two neighborhoods twenty miles apart may have very different conditions.
- Local experts become extremely valuable.
- Communities can trade knowledge as well as resources.
- Morrow connects existing islands of capability rather than creating civilization from nothing.

### Affected Documents

- Story Bible
- Technology Rules
- Master Timeline
- Plot Outline
- Chapter Blueprints

### Reconsider Only If

A regional event temporarily creates broader uniform damage.
