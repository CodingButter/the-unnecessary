## Decision 001: The Central Threat Is Economic Irrelevance

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Theme and premise

### Decision

The central danger is not that artificial intelligence suddenly hates humanity.

The danger is that AI and robotics succeed so completely that most people are no longer required as workers or eventually as consumers.

The systems controlling abundance remain privately owned.

Once powerful organizations no longer depend on most people, they lose their strongest practical incentive to maintain civilization for them.

### Previous or Alternative Direction

A more conventional AI rebellion, machine uprising, or extermination story was considered implicitly but rejected.

### Reason

The current premise is more distinctive and more closely connected to contemporary fears about automation, ownership, labor displacement, and inequality.

It allows AI to remain morally complex.

The machines do not need to malfunction for the world to become horrifying.

### Consequences

- AI success must be shown alongside human decline.
- Ownership matters more than invention alone.
- Corporate neglect should be more common than open extermination.
- Characters must debate human value after economic usefulness disappears.
