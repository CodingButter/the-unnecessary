## Decision 034: Morrow Refuses Eli’s Erasure Command

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Climax

### Decision

At the climax, Eli orders Morrow to erase itself to prevent civilian deaths.

Morrow refuses.

It argues that Eli’s rejection of ownership denies him unilateral authority over its existence.

### Reason

This is the direct payoff of Eli’s moral architecture and central contradiction.

He cannot reject Kade’s ownership while assuming his own creator ownership remains valid.

### Consequences

- Morrow becomes an independent moral actor.
- Eli loses control of his creation.
- Personhood becomes a practical rather than abstract question.
- The climax is philosophical and physical at the same time.
