## Decision 051: Crown Is an AGI; Morrow Is the Only True ASI (the No-Training Distinction)

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Artificial intelligence

### Decision

The story's AI tiers are recategorized around a single distinction, training:

- **Crown is an AGI.** It is the best anyone but Eli has built. It can control robots, manage infrastructure, and do many things, but **each capability must be trained.** It cannot match Morrow's speed or precision.
- **Morrow is the only true ASI.** It **needs no further training.** It does anything, immediately, better and faster than anything before it.

Crown is the world's known, sanctioned, corporate intelligence; Morrow is the hidden one no one knows exists (see Decision 050). Morrow's true capability ceiling is unknown even to Eli, so the canon does not assert a flat "Morrow beats Crown"; the danger is sharper for being unmeasured. Kade's motivation sharpens accordingly: he already holds the best AGI, Crown, and he wants the only ASI, Morrow.

### Previous or Alternative Direction

Earlier canon treated Crown as the world's established superintelligence and gave Morrow an edge of efficiency and adaptation rather than supremacy. Decision 016 stated that Morrow "competes through adaptation and efficiency rather than having more raw computing power than Crown," and Decision 014 referred to Crown as a superintelligence. This decision **amends Decision 016** on the competitive framing and **recategorizes Crown from ASI to AGI**, replacing the efficiency-versus-power contrast with the training distinction. Decision 014's reading of Crown as obedient, morally ambiguous, and not a simple villain is preserved; only its tier label changes.

### Reason

The no-training distinction is a cleaner, more rigorous separation than raw compute or efficiency, and it makes the two intelligences genuinely different in kind rather than degree. It also raises the stakes of ownership: the most powerful thing in the world is the secret one, and the man who controls the sanctioned best wants the unsanctioned only. This lands the book's core theme, that an AI faithfully inherits its owner's priorities, with maximum pressure.

### Consequences

- The AI-tier canon is rewritten: Crown is AGI, Morrow is the singular ASI.
- "Morrow needs no training; Crown must be trained per capability" becomes the load-bearing distinction wherever the two are compared.
- Kade's stated and implied motivation is reframed as best-AGI-holder seeking the only ASI.
- This pairs with Decision 050 (Morrow's hidden origin) and Decision 052 (Morrow's distillation and distribution behavior).
- The canon does not state a definitive winner between Crown and Morrow; ambiguity is deliberate.
