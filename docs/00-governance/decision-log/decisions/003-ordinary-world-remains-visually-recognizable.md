## Decision 003: The Ordinary World Remains Visually Recognizable

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Setting and visual identity

### Decision

Most ordinary people live in existing houses, apartments, suburbs, towns, and cities that still resemble the 2020s.

The environment is aged, repaired, and unevenly maintained, but not rebuilt into a stylized futuristic slum.

### Previous or Alternative Direction

An early concept used a district called **the Stack**, where poor residents lived in stacked shipping containers.

That concept was rejected.

### Reason

The Stack resembled imagery strongly associated with **Ready Player One** and other established dystopian works.

The recognizable setting also makes the story more immediate.

The horror is stronger when an ordinary street slowly loses support while a wealthy community nearby remains perfectly maintained.

### Consequences

Avoid:

- Shipping-container neighborhoods
- Giant vertical slums
- Neon shantytowns
- Uniform wasteland imagery
- Overdesigned cyberpunk poverty

Prefer:

- Aging houses
- Closed pharmacies
- unsupported smart appliances
- dark cellular towers
- failing streetlights
- patched roads
- improvised local networks
- old vehicles serving as battery storage

### Affected Documents

- Narrative Brief
- Story Bible
- Plot Outline
- Future location documents
- Future Style Guide

### Reconsider Only If

A specific location naturally uses repurposed containers for a practical reason. It must not become the defining visual identity of the wider population.
