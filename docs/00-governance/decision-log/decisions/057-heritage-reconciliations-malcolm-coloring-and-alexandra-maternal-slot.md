## Decision 057: Heritage Reconciliations -- Malcolm Mercer's Coloring Made Coherent, Alexandra Kade's Maternal Slot Ratified Open

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Character canon and heritage

### Decision

The cast-wide heritage pass (which assigned every human profile a stated Heritage field, Detroit-accurate and class-not-race) surfaced two flags it refused to settle. The author delegated both. Both are now resolved.

**1. Malcolm Mercer's coloring is rewritten to a coherent light-skinned African-American.** The Mercers are deliberately a Black family in the Protected-Wealthy tier, the central "wealthy is not white" cross-cut that keeps the class line from reading as a race line. But Malcolm's profile-level coloring, invented before heritage existed, was "fair, ruddy complexion gone pale and papery; entirely white hair; pale gray-blue eyes," which reads as a white man and contradicted both his assigned African-American heritage and his canonically "warm brown, unambiguously Black" son Jonah (`./mercer-jonah.md`). The coloring is now "a light brown, warm-toned complexion, freckled across the cheekbones... light hazel eyes," and the Heritage line drops its strained apology ("reads as a very light, light-eyed Black man") for a plain statement: a Great Migration family up from the Deep South to River Rouge, light-skinned and freckled from generations of mixed Southern ancestry. This makes the family genetically coherent (Malcolm light brown + Elaine light olive -> Jonah warm brown) and keeps the cross-cut intact. No approved manuscript is affected; Malcolm's appearance lives only in his profile.

**2. Alexandra Kade's maternal heritage is NOT invented; the open slot is ratified.** Her mother is a deliberate, load-bearing open canonical slot. Canon establishes only that the parents' relationship ended several years after her 2021 birth; her own profile writing rules state "Do not invent her mother to fill the gap; the open slot is load-bearing," and the maternal question is tied to a reserved later-book reveal. Inventing a maternal heritage would burn a deliberate canon gap. The flag is therefore resolved by **ratifying the open slot as a settled, deliberate choice**, not by filling it. Her paternal line is white East-Coast American (via Adrian Kade); her fair-to-olive coloring is kept deliberately ambiguous as to the maternal line (consistent with a white or part-Mediterranean, Latina, or mixed mother). The "FLAG" call-to-author tokens are removed and replaced with pointers to this decision.

### Previous or Alternative Direction

Malcolm's coloring previously described a white man's features under an African-American Heritage line, with the Heritage line straining to reconcile the two ("very light, light-eyed Black man"). The alternative was to leave the strained reconciliation, or to reconsider the Mercers' Blackness; both were rejected, the first as incoherent (and a portrait/prose drift risk), the second as discarding the most important class-not-race cross-cut. Alexandra's maternal slot carried a standing "FLAG for the author and a future canon pass." The alternative was to invent a mother to "complete" her heritage; rejected because the open slot is load-bearing and reserved.

### Reason

The heritage spine is class against class, not race against race, and it depends on no tier reading as one color. Malcolm is the wealthy-Black anchor; his appearance had to actually support that, not fight it, and it had to be genetically consistent with his canonical son. The fix is a profile-level coloring revision, exactly the kind of low-stakes, reversible adjustment Decision 056 established for non-manuscript flavor. Alexandra's gap is the opposite kind of fact: a deliberate, protected open thread whose value is precisely that it is unfilled. Handling it correctly means recognizing that the right move is to keep it open, ratify that as a decision, and forbid invention, not to "resolve" it by writing a mother.

### Consequences

- `mercer-malcolm.md`: Coloring block rewritten (light brown, freckled, light hazel); Heritage line simplified; no other section touched. His portrait, and Jonah's (conditioned on Malcolm under the family-conditioned portrait pipeline), are to be re-rendered against the corrected profile.
- `kade-alexandra.md`: Coloring, Heritage, the relationship-section mother note, and the Continuity Anchor are reworded to ratify the open slot and point to this decision; the "FLAG" tokens are removed. No maternal heritage is invented. Her appearance and the reserved Mars/maternal reveals are otherwise unchanged.
- The wealthy-Black-Mercer cross-cut and Alexandra's load-bearing open slot both stand.

### Reconsider Only If

The author rules the Mercers should not be a Black family (which would dissolve the central cross-cut and revert Malcolm's coloring), or decides to canonize Alexandra's mother (which would close the load-bearing slot ahead of its reserved reveal). Both are reversible via git history.
