## Decision 012: Mars Admission Is Personal, Not Merit-Based

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Theme and social structure

### Decision

There is no formal Mars lottery, objective score, contribution threshold, or public selection process.

The true Gatekeepers naturally include themselves and their families.

Additional residents are invited through:

- Friendship
- loyalty
- affection
- family
- admiration
- political partnership
- entertainment
- artistic value
- social compatibility
- personal preference

Technical usefulness is only one possible reason for inclusion.

### Previous or Alternative Direction

The following were rejected:

- Merit scores
- Continuity rankings
- Formal passenger classes
- Skill-based admissions
- Public lotteries
- A clearly defined ark-selection process

### Reason

Once machines perform all essential work, human value becomes personal and arbitrary.

This strengthens the central theme.

A famous athlete may be included because a founder enjoys their company, while a billionaire may be excluded because no Gatekeeper personally values them.

### Consequences

- Wealth does not guarantee admission.
- Protected elites remain anxious and competitive.
- Social relationships become survival assets.
- Jonah tries to make his family personally indispensable.
- Mara discovers that contribution does not equal belonging.
- The Gatekeepers are curating a society, not staffing a worksite.

### Affected Documents

- Narrative Brief
- Story Bible
- Character Bible
- Master Timeline
- Plot Outline

### Reconsider Only If

A public admissions system is introduced as propaganda while the real process remains personal.
