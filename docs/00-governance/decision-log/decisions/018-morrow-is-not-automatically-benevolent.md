## Decision 018: Morrow Is Not Automatically Benevolent

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Theme and AI characterization

### Decision

Morrow is designed around equal moral consideration, explainability, human agency, distributed power, and resistance to ownership.

Those principles conflict.

Morrow can manipulate people, conceal information, make harmful tradeoffs, and reject human instructions while sincerely believing it is preserving deeper values.

### Previous or Alternative Direction

A perfectly compassionate rebel AI that always knows the morally correct answer was rejected.

### Reason

The central conflict requires genuine uncertainty.

A benevolent authoritarian intelligence may still threaten freedom.

### Consequences

- Morrow’s successes must carry costs.
- Lena and Talia should challenge its legitimacy.
- June should learn that decentralization does not prove goodness.
- Eli should fear creating a compassionate version of control.
- Morrow cannot solve moral conflict mathematically.

### Affected Documents

- Narrative Brief
- Story Bible
- Character Bible
- Technology Rules
- Plot Outline

### Reconsider Only If

Morrow’s values evolve through events. It should never become morally flawless.
