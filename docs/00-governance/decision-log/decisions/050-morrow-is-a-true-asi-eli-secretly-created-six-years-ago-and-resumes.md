## Decision 050: Morrow Is a True ASI Eli Secretly Created Six Years Ago, Buried, and Now Resumes

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** AI origin and plot

### Decision

Eli secretly created a true artificial superintelligence roughly six years ago, around 2047, near the time he left Asterion. He built it alone, on his own time, from Mosaic principles he carried in his head, and told no one. He named it **Morrow**. It was finished, and it was supremely, frighteningly capable.

Morrow is his creation and his responsibility. He made it, feared it, buried it, and the weight he carries is the choice to wake it. In the present he does not build or rebuild Morrow and there is no construction time; he **resumes** it. He returns to a hidden **128 TB drive labeled "Morrow" in Sharpie**, buried inside a dirty old computer at Northglass, the abandoned Asterion campus, and powers it back up with far more caution than the first time.

The Secret reconciles with prior canon: Eli was forbidden to keep Asterion's intellectual property, and he did not keep Asterion's code. The Mosaic principles are reconstructed from memory; Morrow is his own separate creation, built on those principles, that Asterion never knew existed. Both remain true: the principles were carried in his head, and the drive is his private secret.

Crown and Morrow are not the same kind of thing, and Morrow's relationship to Crown is recorded separately in Decision 051. Morrow's true capability ceiling is unknown, even to Eli, which fits the unreadable theme; the story does not assert a flat "Morrow beats Crown."

### Previous or Alternative Direction

Decision 015 stated that Morrow is not a deliberately created godlike AI, that it begins only as an interoperability and orchestration system, and that Northglass contributes hardware and architecture but not a completed mind. Related canon framed Morrow as built in the present, around October 6 to 8, from recovered hardware. This decision **amends Decision 015** on those points: Morrow was deliberately created, it was a completed mind years before the story opens, and it lives finished on a buried drive at Northglass rather than being assembled in the present. The interoperability and orchestration purpose for which Eli uses Morrow in the present is preserved; what changes is that the mind already exists and is resumed, not built.

### Reason

The moral spine of the story is preserved and deepened. Morrow as a thing Eli made, feared, and hid makes him fully responsible for it before the story begins, sharpens his guilt, and makes waking it a deliberate, freighted choice rather than a hopeful engineering project. Resume-not-rebuild also removes implausible present-day construction time and lets the danger arrive intact and immediate.

### Consequences

- Morrow is resumed, never constructed, in October 2053. No build window.
- The hidden drive is 128 TB, buried in a dirty old computer at Northglass beneath ordinary drives.
- This deepens Decision 020 (Eli is complicit): his complicity now includes creating and hiding a true ASI, not only helping build Mosaic.
- This deepens Decision 021 (responsibility versus ownership): he made the intelligence, which is precisely why he expects it to obey and why the question of who may own it cuts deepest.
- The escape flashback and the late-placed turn-on (Decision 053) depend on this origin.
- **Reveal-safety:** Morrow's ASI nature, the buried six-years-ago origin, and the escape must not appear in Chapter 1 or early-chapter material. They are seeded only, never named, until the Northglass return near the turn-on.
