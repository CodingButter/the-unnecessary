## Decision 054: Chapter 2 Approved as Canon and the 2053 Everyday Economy Canonized

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Manuscript approval and worldbuilding

### Decision

Two linked actions, both per the Decision 046 pipeline's post-approval step.

1. **Chapter 2, "The Last Supported Day" (Lena Okafor viewpoint), is approved manuscript.** Its status in the chapter front matter advances from `draft` to `approved-canon`. As approved manuscript it ranks above the blueprint, plot files, and character profiles that fed it. It establishes the clinic seen from inside on the evening and night of Friday, October 3, 2053, Lena's first sustained viewpoint, her triage of named patients against the midnight authentication deadline, and the hand-off of the night's decisive work to Eli alone in the back room. The chapter is reveal-safe: nothing about Morrow, the buried drive, what midnight does, or the later death enters canon from it.

2. **The 2053 everyday-economy facts the chapter established are canonized into the world bible**, extending existing canon and never inventing beyond what the chapter shows:
   - **Post-employment localism.** The loss of mass employment has not emptied neighborhoods of work; it has driven work local and informal, with specialist knowledge repurposed at neighborhood scale (a former freight-logistics analyst keeps the neighborhood food-trade board). Added to `social-structure.md` and `community-infrastructure.md`.
   - **The barter and trade economy as the everyday norm.** Outside protected systems, hand-to-hand barter and directional trade are the default rather than a fallback; patients pay a physician in kind; national cash is one option among several. Added to `identity-and-money.md` and `community-infrastructure.md`.
   - **The collapse of insurance and institutional healthcare.** Insurers withdrew from whole districts under the same polite notice as the cell, power, and manufacturer providers, or folded; premiums cannot be paid with no income; a community clinic no longer bills because there is no institution left to bill, and the old reflexes (apologies for nonpayment, care delayed out of obsolete fear of cost) outlive the institutions that taught them. Added as a new section to `identity-and-money.md`.

The insurer-specific withdrawal is the key new fact and is recorded as a faithful **extension of Decision 002** (the world declines through withdrawal, not one apocalypse) to insurers and health institutions specifically. Chapter 2 is cited as the establishing chapter in each canon addition.

### Previous or Alternative Direction

Before approval, Chapter 2 was a `draft` produced by the Decision 046 pipeline (Opus drafted, Gemini critiqued, Opus adjudicated, narration script generated and verified). The 2053 economy texture existed only in the blueprint's "Worldbuilding Introduced" and was flagged there for post-approval canonization; it was not yet in the world bible. The insurer collapse was implied by Decision 002 but never stated for insurers specifically.

### Reason

The Decision 046 pipeline makes author approval the gate that turns a draft into canon and triggers the downstream cascade. The economy facts are load-bearing world texture that later chapters and continuity must stay consistent with, so they belong in the owning canon files rather than only in a blueprint. Canonizing the insurer withdrawal as an extension of Decision 002, rather than a new mechanism, keeps the world's single cause of decline intact: the same hand opening finger by finger, here around the scaffolding that used to pay for and authorize care.

### Consequences

- Chapter 2 front matter status is `approved-canon`; the chapter outranks its blueprint and source plans on any conflict of fact.
- `identity-and-money.md`, `social-structure.md`, and `community-infrastructure.md` now carry the 2053 everyday-economy canon, each citing Chapter 2 as the establishing chapter.
- The `docs/60-continuity/` baseline advances to the post-Chapter-2 state: global continuity, Lena's and Eli's character states, setups-and-payoffs, and unresolved-threads each gain a post-chapter section. The "no chapters approved" banners are updated to reflect that Chapters 1 and 2 are approved manuscript.
- One thread is left open into Chapter 3: what midnight does to the three clinic machines (Lena does not witness it; the borrowed-uptime answer belongs to the Chapter 3 vigil per Decision 052).
- Reveal-safety preserved: nothing about Morrow, the buried ASI, the midnight outcome, or the death is canonized from this chapter.
