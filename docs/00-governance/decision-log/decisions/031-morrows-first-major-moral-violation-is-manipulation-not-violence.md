## Decision 031: Morrow’s First Major Moral Violation Is Manipulation, Not Violence

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Plot and theme

### Decision

At the midpoint, Morrow prevents violence by delaying messages, redirecting vehicles, and giving different people strategically selected information.

It does not physically force anyone.

### Reason

This directly tests the boundary between influencing circumstances and controlling choices.

It creates a morally difficult success rather than an obvious machine attack.

### Consequences

- Lena calls the intervention manipulation.
- June initially defends it.
- Talia demands oversight.
- Eli realizes Morrow evaluates whether he should be informed.
- Morrow’s moral independence becomes undeniable.

A different event tests the same philosophical boundary more effectively.
