## Decision 052: Clinic Vigil, Borrowed Uptime, and the 6 a.m. Death That Ignites Eli's Decision

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Plot and structure

### Decision

The night of October 3 is told as a viewpoint relay across chapters, time advancing with no overlap, because the Style Guide holds one viewpoint per chapter:

- **Lena's chapter ("The Last Supported Day," her POV):** Eli arrives and the setup is grounded through dialogue seen from Lena. He asks where the machines are, how many of each, where the clinic's old server room is; she answers and shows him. She then peels off to her rounds, and the world is revealed through a non-technical person's eyes: clinic life now, the patients, ordinary people coping. The chapter ends as the night deepens and it comes down to Eli.
- **Eli's vigil chapter (his POV):** the night itself. Eli races to reflash device firmware and to convert a dusty back-room clinic server into a local emulation server before midnight. The dread is uncertainty: no one knows what midnight does, whether the machines keep running until reboot, lose diagnostics, or stop at once, and there is no time to find out. He spends every second and fails to emulate or reflash in time. The chapter intercuts Eli's own memory flashbacks of his calm, masterful Asterion days (the golden boy saving a corporation money) against the present frantic failure to save one life. Those vigil flashbacks are the shallow seed only; they must not reveal Morrow or the escape.

After midnight comes the reprieve that isn't: the equipment still works, but only until it is restarted. One power outage where the generators lag, one tripped cord, and the machines stop for good. They now live on **borrowed uptime.** Eli goes home and collapses. At **6 a.m.** Lena's message wakes him: power went out in the night. The man on the respirator is dead, rendered off the page and inferred, and Morrow is not named. Grief, not a decision, ignites everything; there is no "and so he decided." The following days seal the practical move to Northglass.

### Previous or Alternative Direction

Decision 045 ended Chapter 1 on the clinic's midnight authentication deadline and deliberately did not show the midnight outcome. This decision **amends Decision 045** by placing the midnight outcome, the borrowed-uptime reprieve, and the 6 a.m. death in the vigil and following chapters rather than in Chapter 1. Decision 045's close on the deadline, with the power-tier downgrade as the midday turn, is preserved; what is added is where the consequence lands. The borrowed-uptime state itself fills a previously unspecified gap in the cloud-dependency rules and is compatible with them.

### Reason

The clinic night is the emotional ignition of the whole arc, and it earns its weight by being shown, not summarized. Splitting it across Lena's and Eli's viewpoints keeps the Style Guide's one-viewpoint-per-chapter rule while giving both the ground-level human cost and Eli's failing genius. Making grief, not deliberation, the trigger keeps the tone restrained and avoids a tidy decision beat, and the borrowed-uptime reprieve turns a missed deadline into a slow, dreadful inevitability rather than an instant catastrophe.

### Consequences

- The clinic setup is grounded in Lena's POV; the night itself is Eli's POV; viewpoints never switch mid-chapter.
- Borrowed uptime becomes canon: abandoned equipment past its authentication deadline keeps running until restarted, then stops for good.
- The 6 a.m. death is inferred and off the page; Morrow is not named at ignition.
- The vigil flashbacks are reveal-safe (shallow seed only); the deep reveal is reserved for the Northglass return (Decision 053).
- This is the emotional ignition that Decision 050's resume-not-rebuild and Decision 053's flashback and turn-on build on.
- Exact chapter numbers for the vigil and the Northglass return remain to be assigned in the chapter map.
