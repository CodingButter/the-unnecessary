## Decision 002: The World Declines Through Withdrawal, Not One Apocalypse

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Worldbuilding and tone

### Decision

Civilization does not collapse because of one war, pandemic, machine revolt, or sudden catastrophe.

The decline happens through accumulated service withdrawal.

Internet providers leave unprofitable areas.

Cell towers stop receiving maintenance.

Hospitals lose software support.

Utilities lower service priority.

Roads, water systems, and public institutions deteriorate unevenly.

### Previous or Alternative Direction

A conventional post-apocalyptic collapse with obvious ruins, widespread gangs, and one identifiable disaster was rejected.

### Reason

Administrative abandonment is more unsettling because it remains familiar.

People do not receive an announcement that civilization has ended.

They receive professional notices explaining that service will no longer be provided.

### Consequences

- The world should remain inhabited and socially complex.
- Governments still exist.
- Corporations still issue contracts and notices.
- Modern equipment often remains physically present.
- Decline must appear gradually through ordinary systems failing.

### Affected Documents

- Narrative Brief
- Story Bible
- Technology Rules
- Master Timeline
- Plot Outline

### Reconsider Only If

A future book introduces a specific disaster as a consequence of the existing decline. It should not replace the established cause of the world’s condition.
