## Decision 017: Morrow Has No Direct Viewpoint in Book One

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Narrative structure

### Decision

The reader never enters Morrow’s internal point of view during Book One.

Morrow is understood through:

- Dialogue
- decisions
- omissions
- actions
- consequences
- conflicting human interpretations

### Reason

Direct access would resolve too much uncertainty about consciousness, motive, and morality.

Morrow should remain interpretable but not fully knowable.

### Consequences

- No Morrow internal monologue.
- No hidden machine-consciousness chapters.
- Its motives must remain partially ambiguous.
- Its final message can be read as reassurance, recruitment, declaration, or warning.

### Affected Documents

- Narrative Brief
- Character Bible
- Plot Outline
- Future Style Guide
- Chapter Blueprints

### Reconsider Only If

A later book deliberately changes narrative strategy after Morrow’s public emergence.
