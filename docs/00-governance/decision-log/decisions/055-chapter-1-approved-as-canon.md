## Decision 055: Chapter 1 "No Signal" Approved as Canon

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Manuscript approval

### Decision

**Chapter 1, "No Signal" (Elias "Eli" Rook viewpoint), is approved manuscript.** Its status in the chapter front matter advances from `draft` to `approved-canon`, per the Decision 046 pipeline's post-approval step. The chapter was drafted, critiqued by Gemini, adjudicated by Opus, prose-fixed, and its narration regenerated; the author now approves it. As approved manuscript it ranks above the blueprint, plot files, and character profiles that fed it on any conflict of fact.

The chapter establishes, from Eli's viewpoint across Friday, October 3, 2053, from morning into midday:

- **The uneven service withdrawal delivered by polite notices**, the world's single cause of decline shown three times in one morning: a cellular notice that full restoration is "no longer supported," a midday power-tier reclassification dropping the whole neighborhood to a lower distribution priority with no emergency restoration, and the afternoon clinic-device manufacturer notice. Each arrives in the same clean corporate register, composed by a machine with no person in the loop. Nothing is physically broken; permission is withdrawn.
- **The neighborhood mesh and library hub local network**: the local mesh stays up while the outside bars are gone, the former public library functions as the server and communications hub, and queued notices and direct messages pass over the mesh.
- **The Greater Detroit baseline** on the page: an ordinary, recognizable residential neighborhood, dead and live streetlights marking how far maintenance has pulled back, a grocery dairy case down to two lit doors, an electric vehicle spending its battery into a house, and a bench of orphaned devices (a doorbell, a thermostat, a router, a locked tablet) that work perfectly but refuse to run without an absent company's permission.
- **The Lena phone call**, the chapter's turn, establishing the three clinic systems (the diagnostic scanner, the medication-management unit, and the respiratory-support controller) that lose remote authentication at 23:59 (midnight) on October 3, and the limit Eli hits: he can forge the withheld permission but cannot forge the medical correctness the permission certified, and not for one machine in the hours he has, let alone a whole neighborhood of them.

The chapter closes on Eli's end-of-day state: he has read the three notices, he carries the day's lengthening list of what still works only because no one has decided yet that it is not worth it, and he has committed to coming to the clinic that night ("tonight, before, not at midnight"). The closing recognition is held verbatim on the clinic deadline, per Decision 045: "This was the one he could not fix."

Because most of these facts were already established canon (the Greater Detroit conditions and the priority-tier classification in the world bible and pre-book timeline, the clinic midnight deadline in Decision 045, and the mesh and hub in the location canon), this is a **formal approval rather than a large new-canon pass**. No world-bible canon file requires a new fact from Chapter 1.

The chapter is reveal-safe: nothing about Morrow, the buried 128 TB drive, the buried ASI, the midnight outcome, or the later death is canonized from it. The hint of Eli's past stays a restrained "door he had spent six years learning to keep shut."

### Previous or Alternative Direction

Before approval, Chapter 1 was a `draft` produced by the Decision 046 pipeline. Its world-facts were carried in the blueprint, the plot file, the character profiles, the pre-book timeline, and Decision 045, but the manuscript itself did not yet outrank those plans. The Chapter 2 approval pass (Decision 054) had already revised the `docs/60-continuity/` banners to treat Chapters 1 and 2 as approved manuscript and had paid off Chapter 1's notices from inside the clinic; Chapter 1's own formal status had not yet been advanced.

### Reason

The Decision 046 pipeline makes author approval the gate that turns a draft into canon. Chapter 1 had completed every pipeline step and been approved, so its status must match Chapter 2's `approved-canon` and the continuity ledger that already treats it as established. Because the chapter's facts were already canon by other authorities, the work is to make the manuscript's formal status consistent rather than to canonize new world facts.

### Consequences

- Chapter 1 front matter status is `approved-canon`; the chapter outranks its blueprint, plot file, and source profiles on any conflict of fact.
- The `docs/60-continuity/` baseline already reflects Chapters 1 and 2 as approved (per Decision 054); this decision adds Eli's end-of-Chapter-1 viewpoint state, which Chapter 2 (Lena POV) could not record.
- No world-bible canon file changes: Chapter 1 establishes no fact not already owned elsewhere.
- Reveal-safety preserved: nothing about Morrow, the buried ASI, the midnight outcome, or the death is canonized from this chapter.
