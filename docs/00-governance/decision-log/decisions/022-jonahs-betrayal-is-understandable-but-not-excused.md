## Decision 022: Jonah’s Betrayal Is Understandable but Not Excused

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Character and plot

### Decision

Jonah exposes Morrow because he sees it as a way to make his family personally indispensable to Asterion and secure Mars access.

He does not intend to destroy Eli’s community.

He understands social power but overestimates his own place within it.

### Previous or Alternative Direction

Jonah as a greedy, malicious, or secretly corporate villain was rejected.

### Reason

His betrayal is stronger when it comes from love, fear, ambition, and self-deception.

### Consequences

- Jonah must remain sympathetic.
- His family does not automatically approve his actions.
- His later assistance does not erase the betrayal.
- His belief that he can negotiate with Kade should deteriorate gradually.

### Affected Documents

- Story Bible
- Character Bible
- Master Timeline
- Plot Outline

### Reconsider Only If

His method of exposure changes. His emotional logic should remain complex.
