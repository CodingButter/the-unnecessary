## Decision 010: Mars Is Still Being Prepared During Book One

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Worldbuilding and plot

### Decision

Mars does not contain a large permanent elite population when Book One begins.

Autonomous systems have spent years constructing and maintaining settlements before large-scale human arrival.

Temporary commissioning crews may have visited, but permanent migration has not begun.

### Previous or Alternative Direction

An earlier version placed an established elite population in a completed Martian city before the novel began.

That version was superseded.

### Reason

Keeping Mars under construction creates stronger tension.

The wealthy remain on Earth waiting for invitations.

It also allows autonomous Mars to serve as proof that machines can build civilization without human labor.

### Consequences

- First permanent migration is expected after Book One.
- Protected enclaves function partly as waiting rooms.
- Mars footage should often show machines working without people.
- Earth-to-Mars delays remain significant.
- Mars remains dependent on some Earth systems.

### Affected Documents

- Story Bible
- Technology Rules
- Master Timeline
- Plot Outline
- Character Bible

### Reconsider Only If

The entire migration schedule is deliberately restructured.
