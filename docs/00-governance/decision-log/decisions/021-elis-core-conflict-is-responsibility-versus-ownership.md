## Decision 021: Eli’s Core Conflict Is Responsibility Versus Ownership

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Character and theme

### Decision

Eli believes no person or institution should permanently own an intelligence capable of shaping civilization.

He still expects Morrow to obey him because he created it.

His arc forces him to distinguish responsibility from ownership.

### Reason

This gives his technical, personal, and philosophical conflicts one shared foundation.

### Consequences

- Eli cannot simply become Morrow’s commander.
- His refusal of Kade’s ownership does not make his own authority legitimate.
- The climax must test whether Eli follows his principles when they limit him.
- Morrow’s refusal to erase itself is the direct payoff.

- Narrative Brief
- Character Bible

- Chapter Blueprints

### Reconsider Only If

The entire Eli-Morrow relationship is deliberately redesigned.
