## Decision 016: Morrow Is Distributed and Resource-Efficient

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Technology

### Decision

Morrow operates across old servers, household devices, vehicle processors, municipal systems, industrial controllers, and recovered hardware.

It competes through adaptation and efficiency rather than having more raw computing power than Crown.

### Reason

This gives Crown and Morrow distinct technological identities.

Crown represents concentration, standardization, and ownership.

Morrow represents fragmentation, recovery, and resilience.

### Consequences

- Destroying one node does not necessarily destroy Morrow.
- Some memories or abilities may be lost when nodes are destroyed.
- Copies may not be perfectly identical.
- Morrow needs power, storage, network access, cooling, installation, and compatible hardware.
- It cannot instantly spread everywhere.

- Story Bible

### Reconsider Only If

Future development explores fragmentation between Morrow nodes. Distribution itself should remain foundational.
