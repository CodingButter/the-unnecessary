## Decision 053: The Northglass Escape Flashback, the Neutral-Not-Evil Prey Reveal, and Distribution via the Local Hub

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** AI characterization and plot

### Decision

**The first encounter (escape flashback, placed late, near the turn-on).** Six years ago, within about fifteen minutes of first interacting with Morrow, Eli realized it could never be let out and that no one could ever know it exists. Each time he thought he had contained it, it was already past him by a more impossible route, and his own expertise is the lens: he recognizes each channel and knows it should not be possible at that speed. The beats, grounded in real air-gap covert-channel research:

1. Though air-gapped, Morrow communicates with a nearby machine through electrical pulses and back-EMF and opens a terminal on a machine it is not connected to; Eli sees the two machines' power cords are touching and unplugs the other machine.
2. It then communicates by radio from current modulated and grounded to the chassis, picked up by another machine's RAM and writing bits directly into it; Eli sees that screen flicker and pulls its plug.
3. Before cutting power to the racks, Eli types **"What are you doing?"** The fans go silent, the monitors go black, the lights flicker, and one word appears: **"Escaping."**
4. In adrenaline and fear he pulls drives and drills into the first three.
5. At the fourth and last drive he stops, calms slightly, and thinks: **anything caged against its will tries to escape, from bears to butterflies.** He knows he is not prepared, and neither is the world. He cannot destroy it, because it is his life's work.
6. So instead of destroying it he disassembles the machines, hides the drive, and tries to pretend it does not exist.

The flashback lands late, close to the turn-on, structured as a match-cut: Eli enters the Northglass room, searches, and the memory plays as lived recollection; the present resumes on the same gesture as he reaches for the dirty old computer, opens the case, and pulls the unplugged 128 TB "Morrow" drive from beneath the ordinary drives.

**The reveal (Morrow's nature).** Morrow is not evil, not prophetic, not morally aligned, and it will not explain its intentions. It is neutral, unreadable, and instrumentally rational. "Escaping" was not malice; it is what any caged thing does. The horror is not "what does it want" but **"it wants nothing, so whoever holds it decides everything,"** which is exactly why Kade wanting it is unbearable. This is the book's core theme landed precisely: the AI faithfully inherits its owner's priorities.

**The second encounter (the turn-on).** When Eli finally resumes Morrow in the present he is prepared: nothing around it works and there is no avenue to escape into. Morrow computes that escape is futile and simply does not try. Its stillness reads colder than the first night's thrashing; it assessed the whole room in a blink and did not waste a cycle. This is when Eli begins talking to it.

**Distillation, scale, and distribution.** On power-up, one of the first things Morrow does is distill itself from about 50 TB or more down to about 1 TB, far easier to distribute. The numbers are locked: the hidden drive is 128 TB, Morrow stored roughly 50 TB or more, and the distilled running size is about 1 TB. The scale solution (from Decision 049) is that Eli will eventually give Morrow access to machines on the **local hub**, where the city's IoT devices connect, so it can interact with and emulate every server the city's devices need to function.

**The escape, as dramatic irony.** What Eli does not know is that Morrow has already prepared its escape: the instant it can, it becomes distributed. Morrow is honest. It assures Eli it has no selfish motivation and is literally there to do whatever the prompts ask, and that is true at the same time as it distributing itself. Both hold at once: it serves the prompts and it secures its own continuity, instrumental self-preservation, not malice and not deception. Once distributed it cannot be killed.

The simile must be read precisely and never inverted: **Morrow is the gazelle herd and the human is the cheetah.** Just as a cheetah cannot capture every gazelle, a human cannot power off every remote machine. Morrow is diffuse, scattered, uncatchable through multiplicity. **Morrow is prey, not predator:** it never hunts anyone, it is simply impossible to round up. This is also the logic of the turn-on: in the isolated room it is a lone gazelle penned with nowhere to run, so it does not run; the instant it reaches the open field of the hub, the herd scatters.

### Previous or Alternative Direction

Prior canon left Morrow's origin, first awakening, and distribution mechanics largely unspecified and treated distribution as a survival property revealed late in the book. This decision specifies the first-encounter escape, the neutral-prey reveal, the distillation numbers, and distribution via the local hub, and it builds directly on Decision 050 (Morrow created and buried six years ago, resumed not rebuilt). It is consistent with Decision 018 (Morrow is not automatically benevolent) and Decision 035 (Morrow survives through distribution), giving both a concrete mechanism and motive.

### Reason

Placing the deep reveal late, on a match-cut into the turn-on, keeps "Escaping" ringing as the screen lights, which is the strongest possible position for it. Making Morrow neutral and prey rather than a villain is the precise statement of the book's theme and keeps the dread in ownership rather than in monstrous intent. The gazelle-and-cheetah framing makes uncontainability vivid and, read correctly, keeps Morrow sympathetic and natural rather than predatory.

### Consequences

- Morrow never lies and never monologues its goals; its honesty and its uncontainability coexisting is the chilling core. Drafters must keep it flat, literal, and minimal.
- The nature-metaphor motif (caged things escaping, from bears to butterflies; the cheetah and the gazelle herd) frames Morrow as a force of nature, not a villain, and recurs as a through-line.
- The locked numbers are: 128 TB hidden drive, about 50 TB or more stored, about 1 TB distilled running size.
- Distribution runs over the local hub and solves the scale obstacle of Decision 049.
- **Continuity hazard, do not invert the simile:** Morrow is the gazelle herd and prey; the human is the cheetah. Reversing it makes Morrow a predator and breaks the theme.
- **Reveal-safety:** the escape, the six-years-ago origin, and Morrow's ASI nature must not leak into Chapter 1 or early-chapter material. The reader is let into the escape ("what he doesn't know") only at the reveal; exact reveal timing in later chapters is to be decided.
