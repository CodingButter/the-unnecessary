## Decision 004: There Is No Universal Name for the Abandoned Population

**Status:** Locked for Current Draft
**Category:** Social worldbuilding

### Decision

The majority of humanity does not become a formally named class.

People continue identifying by city, neighborhood, nationality, culture, religion, family, profession, and community.

Institutions may use bureaucratic descriptions such as:

- Low-service region
- Unsupported municipality
- Non-priority district
- Economically inactive zone

None becomes the universal identity of the population.

### Previous or Alternative Direction

Terms such as **the Unserved**, **the Left Behind**, and a formal class structure were considered.

### Reason

A universal class name makes the world feel artificially organized and too similar to conventional dystopian caste systems.

The decline is messy, regional, and politically inconsistent.

### Consequences

- Do not have ordinary characters casually identify as “the Unserved.”
- Bureaucratic terminology may differ between governments and corporations.
- Some activist movements may create names later, but none begins as universal.
- Planning documents may use analytical categories for convenience, but the manuscript should not treat them as official castes.

### Affected Documents

- Story Bible
- Narrative Brief
- Plot Outline
- Dialogue and terminology

### Reconsider Only If

A later political movement deliberately creates a shared identity as part of the story.
