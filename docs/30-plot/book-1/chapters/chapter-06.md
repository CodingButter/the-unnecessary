# Chapter 6: MOR-0

**Date:** Monday, October 6
**Viewpoint:** Eli
**Primary setting:** Eli’s workshop

### Purpose

Hold Eli's early coordination work on the salvaged orchestration components, while the **creation-of-Morrow** function this chapter originally carried is removed: Morrow is not created, and its resume lands late. Reveal that Eli retained Mosaic principles in his head, without yet exposing the buried project.

### Chapter Movement

Eli begins integrating the recovered low-power orchestration components with his local infrastructure software, trying to make the neighborhood's incompatible systems cooperate.

The hardware alone is not enough, and the work draws on Mosaic principles he was contractually forbidden to retain but carried in his head.

June realizes that Eli remembers far more of the architecture than he has admitted.

The coordination layer successfully interprets several incompatible power protocols. It is plumbing, not a mind, and it is **not Morrow**. (Naming, MOR-0 acronym, and June's "Morrow" pun are struck per the revision; Morrow was named by Eli six years ago and is not introduced here.)

It does not behave like a person, because it is not one.

### Character Movement

Eli edges from repairing abandoned systems toward something more, while the thing he most fears, the buried project, stays unspoken and untouched. (The line he truly crosses, waking Morrow, is held for later.)

### Information Revealed

- Eli secretly retained Mosaic principles in his head and uses them here.
- The salvaged coordination layer's first purpose is translation and coordination.
- Reveal-safe: nothing here exposes that Morrow already exists, is an ASI, or is buried at Northglass.

### Ending Hook

The salvaged coordination layer identifies a battery-routing solution that Eli's own model missed, capable, but ordinary tooling, not the thing he is afraid to wake.
