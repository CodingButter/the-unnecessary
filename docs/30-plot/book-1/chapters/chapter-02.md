# Chapter 2: The Last Supported Day

**Date:** Friday, October 3
**Viewpoint:** Lena
**Primary setting:** Lena’s clinic

### Purpose

Show what technological withdrawal means in human terms, through the eyes of someone who is not an engineer, and hand the night off to Eli.

### Chapter Movement

The chapter is the clinic evening, told entirely from Lena. It opens on the setup and then leaves the technical work behind to follow Lena's rounds, so the world is revealed through a non-tech person's eyes rather than through machinery.

Eli arrives. The setup is grounded through dialogue seen from Lena: he asks her where the affected machines are, how many of each kind, and where the clinic's old server room is. She answers and shows him, and through her telling we learn which three systems lose authentication at midnight and what each one keeps alive.

Lena understands the problem only in the terms that matter to her: which patients cannot wait, which procedures can safely continue and which must stop tonight. She must make those calls without knowing whether the machines will keep running past midnight or stop the moment they are restarted.

She leaves Eli to the back-room work and peels off to her rounds. The clinic and the neighborhood are shown as they are now, through her: the patients, the regular people coping with a world that has quietly stopped supporting them, the small adaptations and indignities of unsupported care. Worldbuilding through a non-tech lens, not through wiring.

The chapter ends as the night deepens. Lena has done what she can for her patients and it is down to Eli now, alone in the back room against a midnight deadline. She does not see what he does there. The viewpoint stays with her, and hands the night forward.

### Character Movement

Lena begins with anger at the companies abandoning the clinic.

She ends the chapter equally angry at a world in which the people she is responsible for now depend on one man's unauthorized work, and on a deadline no one chose.

### Information Revealed

- Lena knows part of Eli’s Asterion history.
- Medical AI can diagnose accurately while remaining inaccessible.
- Unsupported technology creates moral risk even when repaired.
- No one, Eli included, knows what midnight actually does to the machines: whether they keep running until restarted, lose diagnostics, or stop at once.

### Ending Hook

The night deepens, Lena's patients are as settled as she can make them, and it is down to Eli alone in the back room against the midnight deadline.
