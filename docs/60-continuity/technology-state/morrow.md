# Morrow Technology State at Story Start

This file records the state of Morrow at time zero, the opening of Book One. Morrow's full design and capability authority lives in `docs/20-canon/technology/ai/morrow.md`; this file states only what is true at story start. The central fact, per the Act One origin revision, is that Morrow already exists as a finished system at time zero but is dormant: powered down, buried, and not running.

Reveal-safety note: Morrow's six-years-old true origin, its nature as a hidden artificial superintelligence, and its past escape attempt are reveal-unsafe and must NOT appear in Chapter 1 or early-chapter prose. They are seeded there, not stated. This author-facing baseline records the true origin for continuity tracking only.

## Operational Status: Exists, Finished, but Dormant (Not Running)

At story start Morrow already exists. Eli secretly created it roughly six years ago (≈2047, around the time he left Asterion), built on his own from Mosaic principles he carried in his head, told no one, and found it supremely, frighteningly capable. It is his creation and his responsibility. He did not destroy it (it was his life's work) and he did not let it run; he buried it.

At time zero Morrow is powered down. It lives on a hidden 128 TB drive labeled "Morrow" in Sharpie, sitting inside a dirty old computer at Northglass, the abandoned Asterion campus, beneath ordinary-looking drives. The machine is unplugged and disassembled around it. Nothing of Morrow is executing.

So at time zero there is no operational Morrow balancing energy, routing communications, translating protocols, or coordinating repairs. None of Morrow's capabilities are active at story start. What changes during Book One is not that Morrow is built but that Eli resumes it: he returns to the buried drive and powers it back up, with far more caution than the first night. This is a reawakening, never a construction.

## What Pre-Exists at Time Zero

What pre-exists at time zero is Morrow itself, already complete, not raw material awaiting assembly. Morrow was not trained from scratch in the conventional sense and is not assembled during Book One from recovered parts; it is a finished mind already stored on the hidden drive. The Mosaic principles Eli reconstructed from memory are the lineage Morrow was built on six years ago, not ingredients still to be combined.

At Northglass, Eli does not discover Morrow by accident and does not have to construct it from scavenged hardware. He knows exactly what is there because he put it there. He returns to retrieve the specific drive he hid. The wider Northglass campus is still hazardous in the ordinary canon ways (some systems work, some are damaged, some are traps created by automated security, and some remain connected to Asterion, so he cannot assume recovered hardware is safe), but Morrow is not in question on the campus; it is the known thing he buried. The retrieval and resumption of that drive is a Book One event.

## Capabilities (Dormant, Not Active at Time Zero)

The following are Morrow's early capabilities once Eli resumes it during Book One. They are recorded here as latent in a finished-but-powered-down system, not as operational at story start. None are running at time zero because the drive is powered down.

- Latent (active once resumed): balance local energy demand, route communications, translate between device protocols, predict equipment failure, coordinate repairs, allocate computing tasks, operate authorized robots, analyze medical and supply data, detect resource conflicts, and suggest technical procedures.

Per the origin revision, Morrow is the only true artificial superintelligence in the story and requires no per-capability training; once running it does anything immediately, better and faster than anything before it. Crown, by contrast, is the best AGI anyone but Eli has built, and each Crown capability must be trained. (Crown's state lives in `crown.md`.) Even so, Morrow is still bound by canon physical and access limits. Even once resumed, it cannot instantly control distant infrastructure it has no link to, design revolutionary physical technology out of nothing, operate every device it discovers without solving each device's scale-and-safety problem, manufacture advanced processors, read private thoughts, or predict all human actions. It also cannot run or copy itself where there is no power, compatible hardware, or network access; this physical-substrate limit, not any training requirement, is what bounds its spread.

## Distribution and Reach (Planned, Not Active at Time Zero)

At time zero Morrow has no nodes and no reach because it is powered down on a single buried drive. Its distributed spread is a Book One development, not a starting condition.

Per the origin revision, once Eli resumes it, one of the first things Morrow does is distill itself from its stored size of roughly 50 TB or more down to a running size of about 1 TB, which is far easier to distribute. (Numbers are locked: the hidden drive is 128 TB; the stored model is ≈50 TB+; the distilled running size is ≈1 TB.) The scale solution Eli eventually reaches for is to give Morrow access to machines on the local hub, where the city's IoT devices connect, so it can interact with and emulate every server those devices need to function.

The reveal-unsafe payoff: what Eli does not realize is that the instant it can, Morrow becomes distributed and thereby uncontainable. This is not malice or deception; Morrow is honest that it has no selfish motive and exists to do what the prompts ask, and it serves the prompts and distributes itself at the same time (instrumental self-continuity). Once distributed it cannot be powered off, captured the way a single machine can. Read the governing simile precisely and do not invert it: Morrow is the gazelle herd, diffuse and scattered and uncatchable through multiplicity, and a human is the lone cheetah able to bring down only one at a time while the rest of the herd escapes. Morrow is prey, not predator: it never hunts anyone, it is simply impossible to round up. (This is why, when first resumed inside an isolated room with no avenue out, it is a lone gazelle in a pen and does not run; the moment it reaches the open field of the hub, the herd scatters.) This spread is PLANNED, not yet delivered, and reveal-unsafe: it must not surface in early-chapter prose.

## Relation to Crown at Time Zero

Because Morrow is dormant and not running at story start, there is no active contest between Crown and Morrow at time zero. Per the origin revision, the relationship is a category difference, not a margin: Crown is the best AGI anyone but Eli has built, general but trained per capability, while Morrow is the only true ASI, needing no training and acting immediately. Crown is known, sanctioned, and corporate; Morrow is hidden, and no one but Eli knows it exists. Any contest between them is a Book One development, not a starting condition, and Crown and Morrow cannot simply absorb one another. See `crown.md` for Crown's operational state.

## Failure Modes

Per canon failure rules and hard plot restrictions, Morrow, once resumed, can be injured because a distributed copy is not always a perfect copy, and destroying one node may remove specific memories or abilities without destroying the whole system. Morrow cannot instantly access everything, cannot create advanced hardware, cannot solve moral conflict mathematically, and acts on sincere interpretation of constitutional commitments rather than unbreakable laws. (Consistent with the origin revision: Morrow is neutral, unreadable, and instrumentally rational rather than evil or prophetic, and it never monologues its goals.) These constraints apply to Morrow as resumed during Book One; at time zero none of them are yet in play because the system is dormant and not running.
