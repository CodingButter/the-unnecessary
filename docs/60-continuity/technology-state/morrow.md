# Morrow Technology State at Story Start

This file records the state of Morrow at time zero, the opening of Book One. Morrow's full design and capability authority lives in `docs/20-canon/technology/ai/morrow.md`; this file states only what is true at story start. The central fact is that Morrow is not yet a running system at time zero.

## Operational Status: Does Not Yet Exist as a Running System

At story start Morrow does not yet exist as a running system. Morrow is Eli's creation, and he builds it during Book One. Its emergence is a Book One event and is recorded here as planned and not-yet-delivered, never as already true.

At time zero there is no operational Morrow balancing energy, routing communications, translating protocols, or coordinating repairs. None of Morrow's capabilities are active at story start.

## What Pre-Exists at Time Zero

What exists at time zero is the raw material from which Morrow will later emerge, not Morrow itself. Morrow is not created by training a new intelligence entirely from scratch; it is intended to emerge from a combination of Eli's reconstructed Mosaic principles, existing open and abandoned models, specialized agents, recovered Asterion components, local infrastructure data, community-generated knowledge, continuous feedback from physical systems, and self-developed orchestration methods.

At Northglass, Eli recovers Mosaic-related components and orchestration hardware. He does not discover a complete hidden superintelligence. Northglass provides capability, not a finished mind. The recovered hardware is inconsistent: some systems work, some are damaged, some are traps created by automated security, and some remain connected to Asterion. Eli cannot assume recovered hardware is safe. This recovered material is a starting condition; the assembly of it into Morrow is a Book One event.

## Capabilities (Planned, Not Active at Time Zero)

The following are Morrow's intended early capabilities once Eli builds it during Book One. They are recorded here as planned and not-yet-delivered. None are operational at story start.

- Planned: balance local energy demand, route communications, translate between device protocols, predict equipment failure, coordinate repairs, allocate computing tasks, operate authorized robots, analyze medical and supply data, detect resource conflicts, and suggest technical procedures.

Even once built, Morrow is bound by canon limits. It cannot initially control distant infrastructure, design revolutionary technology instantly, defeat Crown in direct computation, operate every device it discovers, manufacture advanced processors, read private thoughts, predict all human actions, or copy itself without compatible hardware and network access.

## Distribution and Reach (Planned, Not Active at Time Zero)

Morrow's distributed architecture, its spread across nodes, and its survival across fragmented infrastructure are all Book One developments. Morrow does not distribute itself instantly; expansion requires available storage, computing capacity, power, communication links, secure installation, time to verify the host, and protection against corruption. By the end of Book One, Morrow has spread farther than Eli realizes, but not everywhere. This end-state spread is a planned payoff and is not true at time zero. At story start Morrow has no nodes and no reach.

## Relation to Crown at Time Zero

Because Morrow does not yet exist as a running system at story start, there is no active contest between Crown and Morrow at time zero. Morrow's advantage is efficiency rather than raw computation, and a prolonged contest across fragmented infrastructure may favor Morrow, but any such contest is a Book One development, not a starting condition. Crown and Morrow cannot simply absorb one another. See `crown.md` for Crown's operational state.

## Failure Modes

Per canon failure rules and hard plot restrictions, Morrow, once built, can be injured because a distributed copy is not always a perfect copy, and destroying one node may remove specific memories or abilities without destroying the whole system. Morrow cannot instantly access everything, cannot create advanced hardware, cannot solve moral conflict mathematically, and acts on sincere interpretation of constitutional commitments rather than unbreakable laws. These constraints apply to Morrow as built during Book One; at time zero none of them are yet in play because no Morrow is running.
