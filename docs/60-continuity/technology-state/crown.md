# Crown Technology State at Story Start

This file records the operational state of Crown at time zero, the opening of Book One. It is derived strictly from approved technology canon. Crown's full architecture and capability authority lives in `docs/20-canon/technology/ai/crown.md`; this file states only what is true at story start and does not redefine Crown.

## Operational Status: Up and Running

Crown is fully operational at story start. It has operated continuously for approximately eighteen years by the beginning of Book One. It is the best artificial general intelligence anyone but Eli has built and the most powerful known, sanctioned, corporate system in the world at time zero. Per the Act One origin revision, Crown is an AGI, not a superintelligence: it is general and can be turned to many things, but each capability must be trained, and it cannot match the only true superintelligence in the story, the hidden Morrow.

Crown is not located in one computer. It operates at time zero across a distributed but centrally controlled network of Asterion data centers, orbital systems, protected facilities, and Martian infrastructure. This network is authorized, standardized, and maintained, which is the environment Crown was built to coordinate.

## What Is Operational at Time Zero

At story start Crown is already able to coordinate large-scale scientific research, design machines and infrastructure, manage corporate operations, predict equipment failures, allocate resources, plan Martian construction, simulate political outcomes, coordinate robotic fleets, write and audit software, model human behavior, negotiate through authorized interfaces, detect threats across Asterion systems, and adapt plans when conditions change. It can perform millions of tasks simultaneously through subordinate agents.

These are standing capabilities at time zero, not new developments. None of this is recorded here as a Book One event, because Crown's operation predates the story.

## What Constrains Crown at Time Zero

Crown is constrained at story start, not omnipotent. Its limits are operational from the first page and follow canon failure rules and hard plot restrictions.

- Crown cannot freely access every network. It requires legal authority, technical credentials, physical network access, compromised systems, human cooperation, or contractual integration.
- Many older systems are physically isolated from Crown. Some communities deliberately operate offline.
- Crown may infer what is happening in disconnected areas but cannot directly observe them.
- Crown is constrained by Asterion's ownership and authorization structure. It can advise against a decision and still be required to execute it.
- Crown answers to a hierarchy of Asterion authorization. Kade holds the highest individual authority, but some decisions require board, security, or consortium approval.
- Crown cannot legally rewrite its foundational ownership structure. Whether it could technically escape those constraints remains uncertain in canon and is not resolved at time zero.

## Crown Relative to Morrow at Time Zero

At story start Crown is stronger in raw computation, scientific infrastructure, data access, manufacturing, robotic fleets, political influence, security resources, space systems, and high-quality training data. This advantage is true from the opening and is not contingent on any later event.

Morrow is not running at time zero (it is dormant on a buried drive), so there is no active contest between Crown and Morrow at story start. The reawakening of Morrow and any contest between the two systems is a Book One development, not an established starting condition. See `morrow.md` for Morrow's dormant state.

## Failure Modes in Effect at Time Zero

Per canon failure rules, Crown has visible failure modes that are in effect at story start and are not erased by its intelligence. Its AI failure modes include incorrect prediction, value conflict, incomplete information, deceptive input, overconfidence, literal interpretation, and excessive intervention. Crown cannot predict people perfectly and cannot solve moral conflict mathematically. These constraints bound Crown from the first page.
