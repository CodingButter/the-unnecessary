# Talia Reed: Character State at Story Start

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

This file records what is true about Talia Reed at time zero, the opening of Book One. It derives strictly from approved canon and records no planned or future event as established fact.

## Identity and Location

Talia Simone Reed is thirty-five years old, born in Detroit, Michigan, and lives in Eli's neighborhood in Greater Detroit.

She is a former public-school science teacher who taught middle-school science before local schools lost stable funding. She stayed after many professional workers relocated.

Her current occupation is community coordinator and local communications organizer.

## Physical and Resource Condition

Talia is five feet eight inches tall with an athletic build and wears her hair in long braids usually tied back. She dresses simply.

She carries an old tablet that holds local records, agreements, supply counts, and contact lists. This tablet is her working instrument as coordinator.

Canon records no injury, illness, or resource shortage specific to her at story start.

## Immediate Situation

Talia operates inside a neighborhood already under sustained infrastructure withdrawal. In the months before Book One the regional power provider classified the neighborhood below protected, industrial, and government zones, and the regional ISP announced it would stop restoring full service to several unsupported neighborhoods.

She runs the community functions she has organized over time: food distribution, emergency classes, medical transport, and neighborhood communications.

She has no relationship with Morrow at time zero, because Morrow does not yet exist. Morrow is built and named only after Eli's later recovery of Asterion hardware.

## Active Goals

Her external goal is to build a durable form of local governance capable of surviving infrastructure withdrawal.

Her internal need, unmet at story start, is to accept that not every emergency can wait for consensus.

## Stance Toward Eli

Talia respects Eli's ability but resents his habit of making urgent technical decisions without community consultation. She believes he hides political choices inside engineering language. There is potential for emotional closeness but no romance at story start.

## Greatest Fear

She fears replacing corporate abandonment with dependence on one brilliant engineer or one powerful intelligence.

## Held Secret at Story Start

Canon establishes that before Book One Talia has privately prepared plans to isolate or destroy parts of Morrow if it begins controlling community systems without consent. At time zero this is a held contingency known only to her. Because Morrow does not yet exist, the plan is preparatory, not active.

## False Belief at Story Start

Talia believes a legitimate process can contain any form of power. Canon frames this as a belief she must later revise. It is recorded here only as her starting state, not as a resolved arc.

## Not Recorded as Occurred (PLANNED, per canon, not yet delivered)

The following are canon-planned developments and are NOT part of Talia's state at time zero:

- Becoming a central organizer during the containment crisis.
- Persuading residents not to surrender Morrow while preventing Eli from deciding alone.
- Any later activation or use of her isolate-or-destroy plan.
