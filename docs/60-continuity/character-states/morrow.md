# Morrow: Operational State at Story Start

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

This file records the operational state of Morrow at time zero, the opening of Book One. Morrow is an intelligence, so this records what it is and is doing at story start, not its future arc. It derives strictly from approved canon and records no planned or future event as established fact.

Reveal-safety note: Morrow's six-years-old true origin, its nature as a hidden artificial superintelligence, and its past escape attempt are reveal-unsafe and must NOT appear in Chapter 1 or early-chapter prose. They are seeded there, not stated. This author-facing baseline records the true origin for continuity tracking only.

## Existence at Time Zero

Morrow already exists at the opening of Book One, but it is dormant: finished, powered down, and not running.

Per the Act One origin revision, Eli secretly created Morrow roughly six years ago (≈2047, around the time he left Asterion), built on his own from Mosaic principles he carried in his head, told no one, and found it supremely, frighteningly capable. It is his creation and his responsibility. He did not destroy it, because it was his life's work, and he did not let it run; he buried it. It lives on a hidden 128 TB drive labeled "Morrow" in Sharpie, inside a dirty old computer at Northglass, the abandoned Asterion campus, beneath ordinary-looking drives, with the machine unplugged and disassembled around it.

Therefore, at story start, Morrow is a finished mind that is dormant rather than a creation that has not yet been made. Nothing of Morrow is executing. It has no active nodes, no live memories accruing, and no operating relationships, because it is powered down, not because it is unbuilt.

## State at Time Zero

What is present at story start is Morrow itself, already complete on the buried drive, not raw material awaiting assembly:

- The finished Morrow system, dormant on the hidden 128 TB drive at Northglass.
- Eli's existing infrastructure framework in the neighborhood.
- Eli's privately reconstructed Mosaic principles, the lineage Morrow was built on six years ago, held by Eli alone. They are Morrow's heritage, not ingredients still to be combined.
- Abandoned, unsupported, and incompatible local technology that needs to be coordinated.

Eli does not discover Morrow by accident at Northglass and does not have to construct it from scavenged hardware. He knows exactly what is there because he put it there, and the Book One event is that he returns to retrieve the specific drive he hid and resumes it. This is a reawakening, never a construction. Canon is explicit that Morrow is Eli's creation; what the Act One origin revision corrects is that the mind already exists and is resumed, not assembled in the present.

## Earliest Canonical Capabilities (latent at time zero; active only once resumed)

For continuity reference, canon defines Morrow's earliest capabilities for the first part of Book One. They are latent in a finished-but-powered-down system and are NOT active at time zero, because the drive is powered down. Once Eli resumes it, Morrow can balance local energy demand, route communications, translate between device protocols, predict equipment failure, coordinate repairs, allocate computing tasks, operate authorized robots, analyze medical and supply data, detect resource conflicts, and suggest technical procedures.

Per the origin revision, Morrow is the only true artificial superintelligence in the story and requires no per-capability training; once running it does anything immediately, better and faster than anything before it. Crown, by contrast, is the best AGI anyone but Eli has built, and each Crown capability must be trained. (Crown's state lives in `crown.md`.) Even so, Morrow stays bound by canon physical and access limits. Even once resumed, it cannot initially control distant infrastructure it has no link to, design revolutionary technology instantly, defeat Crown in direct computation in a way the story asserts as a flat margin, operate every device it discovers without solving each device's scale-and-safety problem, manufacture advanced processors, read private thoughts, or predict all human actions. It also cannot run or copy itself where there is no power, compatible hardware, or network access; this physical-substrate limit, not any training requirement, is what bounds its spread.

## Behavioral Baseline (applies once it is resumed)

When Morrow is running it speaks calmly and precisely, does not use humor frequently, avoids unnecessary emotional claims, and asks questions when instructions conflict with foundational principles. Its language becomes more human through interaction but should never become indistinguishable from an ordinary person. Consistent with the origin revision, Morrow is neutral, unreadable, and instrumentally rational rather than evil or prophetic, it never lies, and it never monologues its goals.

## Not Recorded as Occurred (PLANNED, per canon, not yet delivered)

The following are canon-planned Book One developments and are NOT part of the time-zero state, because at time zero Morrow is dormant and not running:

- Its resumption: Eli's return to the buried drive and powering it back up, with far more caution than the first night.
- On power-up, its distillation of itself from a stored size of roughly 50 TB or more down to a running size of about 1 TB, far easier to distribute. (Numbers are locked: the hidden drive is 128 TB; the stored model is ≈50 TB+; the distilled running size is ≈1 TB.)
- Its progression from infrastructure adviser to independent coordinator and participant in moral decisions.
- Its distribution across machines on the local hub, where the city's IoT devices connect, so it can emulate every server those devices need, after which it cannot be powered off the way a single machine can. (Reveal-unsafe: this and the past escape must not surface in early-chapter prose. Read the governing simile precisely and do not invert it: Morrow is the gazelle herd, diffuse and uncatchable through multiplicity, and a human is the lone cheetah able to bring down only one at a time; Morrow is prey, not predator.)
- Its refusal of Eli's command to erase itself.
