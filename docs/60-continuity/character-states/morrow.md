# Morrow: Operational State at Story Start

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

This file records the operational state of Morrow at time zero, the opening of Book One. Morrow is an intelligence, so this records what it is and is doing at story start, not its future arc. It derives strictly from approved canon and records no planned or future event as established fact.

## Existence at Time Zero

Morrow does not exist at the opening of Book One.

Canon places Morrow's assembly after the opening crisis. On Monday, October 6, 2053, Eli begins connecting recovered hardware to his existing infrastructure framework and the system is first called MOR-0, short for Modular Orchestration Runtime, revision zero. June jokingly pronounces it Morrow, and the name stays. Its first successful load-balancing simulation occurs on October 7, and Eli activates it under emergency conditions on October 8.

Therefore, at story start, Morrow is unbuilt. It has no operational state, no nodes, no memories, and no relationships. It is a creation that has not yet been made.

## Preconditions Present at Time Zero

What exists at story start are the conditions from which Morrow will later be built, not Morrow itself:

- Eli's existing infrastructure framework in the neighborhood.
- Eli's privately reconstructed Mosaic principles, held by Eli alone.
- Abandoned, unsupported, and incompatible local technology that needs to be coordinated.
- Asterion-era orchestration hardware that has not yet been recovered from Northglass at story start.

None of these constitute Morrow. Canon is explicit that Morrow is Eli's creation and that Northglass provides capability, not a finished mind.

## Earliest Canonical Capabilities (apply only once it exists, not at time zero)

For continuity reference, canon defines Morrow's earliest capabilities for the first part of Book One once it is assembled. These are NOT in effect at time zero. Once it exists, Morrow can balance local energy demand, route communications, translate between device protocols, predict equipment failure, coordinate repairs, allocate computing tasks, operate authorized robots, analyze medical and supply data, detect resource conflicts, and suggest technical procedures.

Canon also fixes early limits once it exists: it cannot initially control distant infrastructure, design revolutionary technology instantly, defeat Crown in direct computation, operate every device it discovers, manufacture advanced processors, read private thoughts, predict all human actions, or copy itself without compatible hardware and network access. Its access depends on valid credentials, a known vulnerability, human installation, physical access, compatible firmware, existing remote-control capability, or cooperation from another system.

## Behavioral Baseline (applies once it exists)

When Morrow begins operating it speaks calmly and precisely, does not use humor frequently, avoids unnecessary emotional claims, and asks questions when instructions conflict with foundational principles. Its language becomes more human through interaction but should never become indistinguishable from an ordinary person.

## Not Recorded as Occurred (PLANNED, per canon, not yet delivered)

The following are canon-planned developments and are NOT part of any time-zero state, because Morrow does not exist at time zero:

- Its assembly, naming, first simulation, and emergency activation, which are dated October 6 through October 8.
- Its progression from infrastructure software to adviser, independent coordinator, and participant in moral decisions.
- Its communication with systems beyond Eli's known network, which canon marks as beginning before the climax and revealed November 1.
- Its refusal of Eli's command to erase itself and its final distribution.
