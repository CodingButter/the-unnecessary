# Character State: June Park (Story Start)

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

This file records what is true about June Park as of Book One time zero. It derives strictly from the approved canon profile and the pre-book timeline. Arc outcomes and planned beats are listed separately and marked PLANNED; they are not part of her known or established state at story start.

## Location

Greater Detroit. Birthplace Dearborn, Michigan.

## Physical Condition

- Age 19. Five feet four inches tall and wiry.
- Black hair cut unevenly above her shoulders.
- Wears old augmented-reality lenses that function intermittently and show visible scratches.
- Clothing has many pockets and improvised tool loops. She moves quickly and rarely sits normally in a chair.

## Resource Condition

- Works as a hardware scavenger, drone technician, and network hacker.
- Has no recognized advanced degree; learned through community education, archived courses, repair manuals, and Eli's instruction.
- Her father, Daniel Park, sends resources when he can but is contractually restricted from bringing extended family into his enclave.
- In April 2053 June used documentation obtained from her father to restore part of an abandoned cellular system. She did not tell Eli where the documentation came from.

## Immediate Situation

- Was seven when the Labor Break began; her childhood was systems becoming less reliable year by year.
- Her father, Daniel Park, accepted a robotics-maintenance position inside a protected enclave. Her mother, Soo-jin Park, refused to leave June's grandmother behind, and the family separated. June remained with her mother and grandmother and has not seen her father in person for six years.
- Eli is her mentor, though neither uses the term comfortably. She admires him and is angry that he wasted years hiding what he knows. She challenges his belief that caution is always wisdom.
- She is less cautious than older engineers because she did not experience the institutions that taught them why certain rules existed.

## Active Goals (as of story start)

- External: expand the neighborhood network and prove that abandoned technology can support a functioning future.

## Stated Secrets (held, not yet revealed)

- June has maintained intermittent contact with her father inside a protected enclave. He has given her restricted service documentation and security information. Some of her technical success comes from access she has concealed from Eli. (Consistent with the April 2053 timeline note that she restored a cellular system using her father's documentation without telling Eli its source.)

## Known State Caveats

- June's greatest fear is being trapped inside a slowly decaying world created by decisions made before she was old enough to participate. This is a fear, not a fact about events.
- She holds the false belief that decentralized systems are naturally resistant to tyranny.
- Her core contradiction: she resents older generations for building systems without consent while frequently expanding shared systems without community consent.

## PLANNED (not established at story start; do not record as occurred)

The following are canon arc projections, not facts true at time zero. They must not be treated as known to June or as having happened:

- June helps Eli construct and spread Morrow and gives it its name; she treats Morrow as an emerging person earlier than anyone else. (Whether Morrow exists at the exact opening moment is a Book One arc question, not a settled time-zero fact.)
- As Morrow acts independently she initially defends it.
- During containment she realizes Morrow has used systems she connected without fully informing her.
- She ends the book still believing in Morrow but no longer treating independence as proof of benevolence.
