# Character State: Jonah Mercer (Story Start)

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

This file records what is true about Jonah Mercer as of Book One time zero. It derives strictly from the approved canon profile and the pre-book timeline. Arc outcomes and planned beats are listed separately and marked PLANNED; they are not part of his known or established state at story start.

## Location

Lakeward, a protected enclave. His father, Malcolm, also lives in Lakeward.

## Physical Condition

- Age 39. Six feet tall with a broad, well-maintained build.
- Warm brown eyes, close-cropped black hair, a face people tend to trust quickly.
- Dresses carefully, current and professionally repaired, communicating competence rather than extreme wealth.

## Resource Condition

- Maintains protected Lakeward access for himself and his family.
- His family wealth derives from his father Malcolm's logistics business and early investments in autonomous transportation and Asterion.
- His position depends on remaining personally pleasant and socially necessary, since Crown could perform much of his job.
- Long-term resource security is uncertain, which he experiences as a threat to medicine, security, education, and his children's future.

## Immediate Situation

- Works as a political and corporate relationship manager, arranging agreements between protected enclaves, corporations, and weakened government institutions.
- Married to Celeste Mercer. Two children: Julian, age twelve, and Amelia, age eight.
- His father, Malcolm, suffers from a progressive neurological condition managed by expensive Asterion-linked medical systems. In May 2053 Malcolm experienced a serious progression in this condition, and Jonah became increasingly desperate to secure long-term access to Asterion medicine.
- In August 2053 Jonah received confirmation that the Mercer family is not included in the first permanent migration group, with later consideration described as still possible. He hides this from his family.
- His marriage is loving but strained by his refusal to admit how little control he possesses.

## Active Goals (as of story start)

- External: secure permanent protection and Mars passage for his family.
- Believes Earth's protected enclaves are temporary, that his children will eventually lose their safety, and that Mars may be the only place where his father's treatment can continue indefinitely.

## Stated Secrets (held, not yet revealed)

- Before the events of Book One, a private Asterion contact warned Jonah that later Mars migration waves would be smaller than publicly implied; the Mercer family's Mars expectations were therefore already uncertain. He did not tell Celeste or his father. (Consistent with the August 2053 timeline note that he hides the family's exclusion from the first permanent group.)

## Known State Caveats

- Jonah carries a false belief that enough money, usefulness, and loyalty will eventually make the Gatekeepers honor their implied promises.
- His greatest fears are watching his children move from protected life into the abandoned world, and Eli seeing only a coward in him. These are fears, not facts about events.

## PLANNED (not established at story start; do not record as occurred)

The following are canon arc projections, not facts true at time zero. They must not be treated as known to Jonah or as having happened:

- Jonah discovers Morrow and sees a path to permanent security.
- He exposes limited Morrow performance data to Asterion expecting negotiation; Kade uses Jonah's contact to locate Eli.
- He believes he can mediate, then realizes he was never part of the decision-making process.
- At the climax he chooses between Lakeward access and helping Eli's community survive containment, and chooses to help. This does not erase his betrayal.
- Celeste is not initially aware that Jonah exposes Morrow; her eventual response is anger rather than gratitude.
