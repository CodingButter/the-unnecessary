# Character State: Adrian Kade (Story Start)

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

This file records what is true about Adrian Kade as of Book One time zero. It derives strictly from the approved canon profile and the pre-book timeline. Arc outcomes and planned beats are listed separately and marked PLANNED; they are not part of his known or established state at story start.

## Location

Multiple protected properties, primarily an Asterion-controlled enclave in Colorado.

## Physical Condition

- Age 61. Tall, lean, physically disciplined.
- Appearance maintained through advanced medicine but not made unnaturally youthful. Silver, closely cut hair.
- Movements controlled. Maintains eye contact without dominating through volume.
- Dresses simply in expensive clothing with almost no visible branding.

## Resource Condition

- Founder and executive chairman of Asterion. One of the Gatekeepers.
- Commands extensive corporate resources, protected enclaves, and Mars assets.
- Holds the highest tier of resource access in the story's world.

## Immediate Situation

- Has one adult daughter, Alexandra Kade, age thirty-two, who works in ecological systems design and has rejected a formal role at Asterion. Their relationship is cordial and distant. He has already reserved a place for her on Mars without asking whether she wants it and assumes she will eventually accept.
- Unmarried. Maintains several close friendships, including artists, scientists, and public figures who have no essential role in the Mars mission; he intends their likely inclusion on Mars.
- In July 2053 Crown advised Asterion that Earth instability may threaten the 2054 launch schedule, and Kade ordered additional security planning.
- Views Eli almost as a failed intellectual heir; does not hate Eli and wants him restored to what he considers Eli's proper place.

## Active Goals (as of story start)

- External: integrate Morrow into Crown and complete Mars's independence from Earth. (At time zero this goal is held in principle; the operational pursuit of Morrow is a Book One arc beat, not a completed action.)
- Preserve a functioning future as he defines it, considering himself responsible for continuity rather than for preserving every existing expectation.

## Stated Secrets (held, not yet revealed)

- Crown has repeatedly advised Kade that maintaining a much larger human population on Earth would improve long-term resilience, genetic diversity, scientific creativity, and species survival. Kade has rejected several such recommendations because they would require surrendering control and resources. He publicly frames the small Mars population as a technical necessity, while it is partly a personal preference.

## Known State Caveats

- Kade's deepest fear is dependence, and at a deeper level that history will remember him as someone who could have saved more people and chose not to. These are fears, not facts about events.
- He holds the false belief that control is the necessary price of continuity.

## PLANNED (not established at story start; do not record as occurred)

The following are canon arc projections, not facts true at time zero. They must not be treated as known to Kade or as having happened:

- Kade begins by viewing Morrow as a valuable asset and Eli as a difficult former employee, and offers Eli restoration, protection, and Mars access.
- Eli's refusal transforms the issue into a test of authority.
- As Morrow distributes itself, Kade recognizes the emergence of an intelligence outside Gatekeeper ownership.
- By the end he views Eli's communities as the beginning of a competing civilization.
