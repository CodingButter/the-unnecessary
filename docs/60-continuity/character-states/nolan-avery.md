# Nolan Avery: Character State at Story Start

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

This file records what is true about Nolan Avery at time zero, the opening of Book One. It derives strictly from approved canon and records no planned or future event as established fact.

## Identity and Location

Nolan James Avery is sixty-four years old, born in Detroit, Michigan, and lives in Eli's neighborhood in Greater Detroit.

He is a former electrical-grid engineer who spent thirty-five years maintaining electrical systems. His current role is community power supervisor.

He understands the regional grid more intimately than anyone else in the community.

## Physical and Resource Condition

Nolan is heavyset with a damaged right knee and walks with a metal brace he repairs himself. He has a gray beard, a deep voice, and permanently stained work gloves.

His health is deteriorating more rapidly than he tells anyone, and he knows the community may lose him within a year. At story start this decline is concealed and known only to him.

## Immediate Situation

In February 2053 Nolan warned that the local grid may become unmanageable within a year, and he concealed the speed of his health decline at that time.

He supervises community power inside a neighborhood that has been moved to a lower service tier and that faces ongoing infrastructure withdrawal.

He has no relationship with Morrow at time zero, because Morrow does not yet exist.

## Active Goals

His external goal is to keep the lights on long enough to train people who can replace him.

His internal need, unmet at story start, is to accept that preserving old knowledge may require integrating new intelligence rather than rejecting it.

## Stance Toward Eli and AI

Nolan distrusts AI because he watched companies replace experienced crews with automated management systems that failed in ways younger engineers did not understand. At story start he treats Eli as someone who helped destroy the profession he loved, while recognizing Eli's skill. Mutual respect is something canon develops later, not a starting condition.

He values apprenticeships and practical knowledge and is stubborn, generous, proud, and suspicious of abstraction.

## Greatest Fear

He fears that the community has become dependent on a small number of aging specialists who cannot be replaced quickly enough.

## Moral Boundary

He will not allow critical grid control to become fully automated without a manual fallback. This is his standing position at story start.

## Held Secret at Story Start

His rapid health decline is concealed from the community. He privately believes the community may lose him within a year.

## Not Recorded as Occurred (PLANNED, per canon, not yet delivered)

The following are canon-planned developments and are NOT part of Nolan's state at time zero:

- Becoming one of the first people Eli trusts with Morrow's physical infrastructure.
- Developing mutual respect with Eli.
- Any disclosure of his health decline to the community.
