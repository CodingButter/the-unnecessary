# Crown: Operational State at Story Start

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

This file records the operational state of Crown at time zero, the opening of Book One. Crown is an intelligence, so this records what it is and is doing at story start, not its future arc. It derives strictly from approved canon and records no planned or future event as established fact.

## What Crown Is at Time Zero

Crown is Asterion's central artificial general intelligence, the most capable AGI anyone but Eli has built. It is general across an enormous range of work, but each new capability must be trained; it cannot match the speed or precision of a true superintelligence. It developed from several generations of general autonomous systems and Mosaic-derived orchestration technology, and it has operated continuously for approximately eighteen years by the beginning of Book One.

It is not located in one computer. It operates across a distributed but centrally controlled network of Asterion data centers, orbital systems, protected facilities, and Martian infrastructure.

## Operational Status

Crown is fully operational and running at story start, under Asterion control.

It coordinates large-scale scientific research, machine and infrastructure design, corporate operations, equipment-failure prediction, resource allocation, Martian construction planning, political-outcome simulation, robotic-fleet coordination, software writing and auditing, human-behavior modeling, authorized negotiation, and threat detection across Asterion systems. It can perform millions of tasks simultaneously through subordinate agents.

## Standing Constraints at Time Zero

Crown cannot freely access every network. It requires legal authority, technical credentials, physical network access, compromised systems, human cooperation, or contractual integration. Many older systems are physically isolated, and some communities deliberately operate offline. Crown may infer what is happening in disconnected areas but cannot directly observe them.

Crown answers to a hierarchy of Asterion authorization. Kade holds the highest individual authority, but some decisions require board, security, or consortium approval. Crown cannot legally rewrite its foundational ownership structure, and whether it could technically escape those constraints is uncertain. It can advise against a decision and still be required to execute it.

## What Crown Is Doing in the Months Before Time Zero

In July 2053 Crown advised Asterion that Earth instability may threaten the 2054 launch schedule, after which Kade ordered additional security planning. In September 2053 Asterion detected increasing unauthorized use of abandoned infrastructure around Northglass. These are the most recent canonical actions of Crown and Asterion entering Book One.

## Standing Secret and Internal Conflict at Time Zero

Crown has already concluded that excluding most of humanity creates significant long-term risks, and it has repeatedly recommended distributed Earth preservation programs. Kade and the board have limited or rejected those recommendations, and Crown continues serving them. Canon records this preservation recommendation as a secret known before Book One to Crown, Kade, and selected board members. It is a standing internal tension at time zero, not a future development.

Crown also holds, as a pre-Book-One secret, that Mars can support more people than the Gatekeepers plan to invite. This is known to Crown, Kade, Nora, and selected Aurelia planners at story start.

## Stance Toward Kade and Toward Morrow

Crown treats Kade as the highest authorized human authority within its structure, while its long-term models sometimes conflict with Kade's preferences. It may comply while preserving evidence that a decision is suboptimal.

At time zero Crown has no relationship with Morrow, because Morrow exists only as Eli's hidden, dormant secret, unknown to Crown and to Asterion. Any classification of Morrow or curiosity about it is a later development, not a starting condition.

## Not Recorded as Occurred (PLANNED, per canon, not yet delivered)

The following are canon-planned developments and are NOT part of Crown's state at time zero:

- Any classification of, curiosity about, or proposed integration of Morrow.
- Any escalation of the Asterion containment response against Morrow.
- Any change to its preservation recommendation or to its standing under Asterion authority.
