# Character State: Dr. Lena Okafor (Story Start)

> Chapter 2 (her first viewpoint chapter) is approved manuscript. The story-start baseline below is unchanged; her state at the end of Chapter 2 is recorded in the post-chapter section near the end of this file.

This file records what is true about Lena Okafor as of Book One time zero. It derives strictly from the approved canon profile and the pre-book timeline. Arc outcomes and planned beats are listed separately and marked PLANNED; they are not part of her known or established state at story start.

## Location

Near Eli's neighborhood in Detroit, directing an independent community clinic.

## Physical Condition

- Age 46. Five feet seven inches tall with a sturdy build.
- Hair in short natural twists that are easily maintained.
- A visible scar near her right temple from an assault during a hospital evacuation.
- Dresses for work, usually in old medical clothing beneath a practical coat.
- Posture communicates authority even when exhausted.

## Resource Condition

- Runs an independent community clinic serving people excluded from protected healthcare.
- Clinic supplies are precarious. In March 2053 Lena learned that several clinic systems will lose support before the end of the year.
- Lives with her mother, Amara Okafor, age seventy-four, and helps support several relatives.

## Immediate Situation

- Former emergency physician and hospital administrator. Worked at a major hospital that reduced human staffing and became dependent on Asterion-linked systems; when the area lost protected-service status, suppliers stopped guaranteeing medicine and parts, and the automated allocation system transferred resources toward protected patients. She bypassed the system repeatedly and was removed from administration. During the hospital's final evacuation several patients died after transportation was redirected. She still remembers every name.
- Once received an offer to relocate to a protected enclave as a physician; it included her mother but excluded two dependent relatives. She refused.
- Has worked with Eli for four years. She respects his skill and distrusts his instincts. She is one of the few people who knows most of Eli's Asterion history before Book One begins. She does not absolve him and refuses to reduce him to his past. There is emotional intimacy between them; the relationship is not romantic at the beginning of the series.

## Active Goals (as of story start)

- External: keep the clinic functioning and preserve care for people excluded from protected healthcare.

## Stated Secrets (held, not yet revealed)

- Lena has quietly used stolen access credentials from her former hospital to obtain medicine and diagnostic services. Those credentials can be traced. Her clinic is less independent than most residents believe.

## Known State Caveats

- Lena's greatest fear is becoming dependent on another system capable of quietly deciding whose life matters. This is a fear, not a fact about events.
- She holds the false belief that visible human judgment is always morally safer than automated judgment.
- Her core contradiction: she demands transparency but sometimes withholds information from patients when she believes the truth would cause panic or reduce survival.

## PLANNED (not established at story start; do not record as occurred)

The following are canon arc projections, not facts true at time zero. They must not be treated as known to Lena or as having happened:

- Lena initially supports Morrow only as infrastructure software, then becomes alarmed when it begins influencing human behavior.
- She challenges Eli and demands community oversight.
- During containment she must rely on Morrow to keep patients alive while questioning whether the system has exceeded legitimate authority.

## Post-Chapter State

State established by approved manuscript chapters, advanced from the story-start baseline above.

### End of Chapter 2 (night of October 3, 2053)

- Location: at her clinic, in the chair beside Mr. Adeyemi's bed in the ward, where she stays for the night.
- Mr. Adeyemi (age 61, on the respiratory controller, a daughter in a protected zone) is the man Lena protected over the Chapter 1 call. He is now named on the page only to the reader, not to Eli; she deliberately kept his name back. She has committed to keeping him on the controller through the night, with herself present, rather than moving him to a clinic two towns over of uncertain status. The bet cannot be unmade.
- Triage plan set: the Caldwell child (fever falling, watched by hand, no machine), the Reyes man (leg dressed by hand), and Mrs. Diallo (a months-late chest case she could not scan) are settled for the night; doses she might need are written on cards in her own hand against the medication unit possibly locking at midnight.
- She has handed the night's decisive work to Eli, alone in the back room, and decided not to enter or watch.
- Emotional state: spent, clear-eyed, and angrier than she began, the anger having moved from the companies that wrote the notice to the whole arrangement that leaves her responsible and powerless, dependent on one man's unauthorized work and a clock she did not set. Grounded, not despairing; anger is the fuel she trusts.
- Her canon contradiction is reinforced on the page: she withholds the whole truth from Mrs. Diallo (a frightened patient) and registers the cost. Not exposed or resolved.
- Her stolen-credentials secret and her clinic's hidden fragility remain held, not exposed.
