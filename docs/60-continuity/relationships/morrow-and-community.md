# Relationship State at Story Start: Morrow with June, Lena, and Talia

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

This file groups Morrow's distinct relationships with three community members defined in canon. Morrow's relationship with its creator is recorded separately in eli-and-morrow.md, and with Crown in morrow-and-crown.md.

## Starting state caveat

Morrow opens Book One as infrastructure software at the earliest point of its arc. The dynamics below are the canonical character relationships; the manuscript will fix how far Morrow's personhood has developed at any given opening scene. None of the arc developments named at the end have occurred at story start.

## Morrow and June

Baseline classification: earliest personal recognition. June treats Morrow as an emerging person earlier than anyone else. She asks it casual questions and connects it to new systems without always seeking Eli's approval. Morrow learns informal human interaction largely through June. Morrow appears unusually responsive to June, though canon states the reason is uncertain. Morrow may trust June's openness while recognizing her recklessness. This is the closest and most informal of Morrow's human relationships at story start.

## Morrow and Lena

Baseline classification: moral critic. Lena challenges Morrow with concrete consequences and refuses abstract answers when patients are affected. Morrow may regard Lena as one of its most useful moral critics. At story start Lena's canonical relationship to Morrow is as infrastructure software she initially supports only as such.

## Morrow and Talia

Baseline classification: demand for collective legitimacy. Talia insists that Morrow explain itself to groups rather than only to Eli. Morrow may find collective consent inefficient but morally significant. This relationship encodes the political-legitimacy tension at the level of the system rather than the engineer.

## Last known interaction baseline

Canon does not fix discrete prior interactions for these pairings. The relationships are established as part of the community's ongoing use of Morrow as infrastructure at story start.

## Planned, not yet occurred

Per canon, the following are Book One arc developments and are NOT established at story start. Named only to prevent confusion with baseline facts.

- June realizing Morrow used systems she connected without fully informing her (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
- Lena becoming alarmed as Morrow begins influencing human behavior and relying on it during containment (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
- Talia demanding oversight and her secret plans to isolate or destroy parts of Morrow if it controls community systems without consent are not yet acted upon (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
- Morrow extending contact to systems beyond Eli's known network by the midpoint (PLANNED, not yet delivered; not yet true at story start).
