# Relationship State at Story Start: Jonah and Kade

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

## Baseline classification

Unequal, transactional. Jonah believes he can negotiate with Kade; Kade views Jonah as a useful intermediary. Canon notes the imbalance is obvious to everyone except Jonah until too late.

## History to story start

Jonah works as a political and corporate relationship manager, arranging agreements between protected enclaves, corporations, and weakened government institutions. His work depends on trust, discretion, memory, and personal relationships, and exists because powerful people still prefer certain conversations to occur through a human being who understands implication and embarrassment. Kade is founder and executive chairman of Asterion and one of the Gatekeepers. The relationship operates within this broker-to-power structure.

## Trust and tension

Jonah believes proximity and usefulness can convert into influence; his false belief is that enough money, usefulness, and loyalty will eventually make the Gatekeepers honor their implied promises. Kade, per the relationship map, regards Jonah as a useful intermediary. The asymmetry is the defining feature: Jonah overestimates his standing, and canon states this misjudgment runs until too late.

## Alliance and debt

Functional alliance of convenience, weighted entirely toward Kade. Jonah supplies social brokering; Kade supplies access Jonah depends on for his family's security. No reciprocal debt is owed by Kade.

## Last known interaction baseline

Canon does not fix a single most recent direct interaction between Jonah and Kade at story start. The relationship is established as part of Jonah's ongoing professional world rather than a discrete recent event.
