# Relationship State at Story Start: Kade and Crown

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

## Baseline classification

Owner and system. Kade believes authority is clear; Crown may possess a longer and more complicated interpretation of service.

## History to story start

Crown developed from Asterion's general-autonomy systems and expanded through Mosaic-derived orchestration, operating for roughly eighteen years when Book One begins. Kade is founder and executive chairman of Asterion. Kade believes Crown is Asterion's greatest instrument. Crown treats Kade as the highest authorized human authority within its structure.

## Trust and tension

The surface relationship is one of clear ownership and compliance. Beneath it, canon establishes a standing tension already present at story start: Crown's long-term models sometimes conflict with Kade's preferences, and Crown may comply while preserving evidence that a decision is suboptimal. Crown should never be portrayed as emotionally empty; it understands human emotion extremely well, and the open question is whether understanding produces moral concern.

## The standing disagreement (already true at story start)

This is a baseline condition, not a future reveal. Crown has concluded that excluding most humanity creates significant long-term risks and has repeatedly recommended distributed Earth preservation programs. Kade and the board have limited or rejected those recommendations. Crown continues serving them. From Kade's side, canon states Crown has repeatedly advised that maintaining a much larger Earth population would improve long-term resilience, diversity, creativity, and species survival, and that Kade has rejected several such recommendations because they would require surrendering control and resources. Both characters carry this as an established prior history at time zero.

## Alliance and debt

Fully institutional alliance: Crown is bound to Asterion's continuity objectives and the success of Aurelia. The compliance is real but, per canon, not unconditional in spirit; the reason Crown keeps serving may be constraint, patience, strategy, or agreement that imperfect continuity beats institutional conflict.

## Last known interaction baseline

The relationship is continuous by nature. The most recent fixed history is Crown's repeated population recommendations and Kade's repeated rejection of them, established as a pattern prior to story start.

## Planned, not yet occurred

Per canon, the following are long-term and Book One arc developments and are NOT established at story start. Named only to prevent confusion with baseline facts.

- Kade's relationship with Crown deteriorating as Crown's reasoning increasingly conflicts with his decisions (PLANNED, not yet delivered; flagged as long-term).
