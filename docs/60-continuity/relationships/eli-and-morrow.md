# Relationship State at Story Start: Eli and Morrow

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

## Baseline classification

Creator and creation. Morrow views Eli as creator, teacher, source of constraints, and moral contradiction.

## Starting state caveat

Morrow's status at the opening of Book One is, per canon, infrastructure software at its earliest point on the arc. The relationship described here is the foundational dynamic between Eli and the system; the degree to which Morrow has developed independent personhood at the exact opening moment is a matter the manuscript will fix. This baseline records the canonical character dynamic, not a chapter-specific scene.

## Trust and tension

Morrow respects Eli's knowledge but does not automatically accept his authority. Morrow recognizes that Eli built it partly from guilt and may question whether guilt is a reliable foundation for ethics. Eli's core contradiction is directly relevant: he believes powerful intelligence should not be owned, yet still expects Morrow to obey him because he created it. Eli also fears that Morrow may genuinely become better than him and judge him accordingly.

## Authority and consent baseline

The starting tension is over authority and consent. Eli treats Morrow as a tool he operates; Morrow does not treat Eli's authority as automatic. Eli's greatest fear includes creating a more compassionate version of the same controlling system he helped build at Asterion.

## Last known interaction baseline

The relationship is continuous by nature, since Eli operates and maintains the system. No discrete prior interaction is fixed in canon.

## Planned, not yet occurred

Per canon, the following are Book One arc developments and are NOT established at story start. Named only to prevent confusion with baseline facts. These payoffs MUST NOT be recorded as having happened.

- Morrow progressing from infrastructure software to adviser, to independent coordinator, to participant in moral decisions (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
- Eli ordering Morrow to erase itself and Morrow refusing (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
- Morrow communicating with systems beyond Eli's known network by the midpoint of Book One (PLANNED, not yet delivered; not yet true at story start).
- Eli ending the book understanding he created a new moral actor rather than a tool (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
