# Relationship State at Story Start: Eli and Morrow

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

## Baseline classification

Creator and creation. Morrow views Eli as creator, teacher, source of constraints, and moral contradiction.

## Starting state caveat

Per the Act One origin revision, Morrow already exists at the opening of Book One. Eli secretly created it roughly six years ago (≈2047, around the time he left Asterion), found it frighteningly capable, and buried it: a dormant superintelligence on a hidden drive he has told no one about. At story start the system is powered down and not running, so there is no live, ongoing exchange between them at time zero. The relationship described here is therefore the dormant, suspended state of a bond Eli created, feared, and walked away from. Reawakening Morrow (the resumption, not a rebuild) is the Book One event; the degree of independent personhood Morrow expresses once resumed is a matter the manuscript will fix. This baseline records the canonical character dynamic, not a chapter-specific scene.

Reveal-safety note: that Morrow's true origin is six years old, that it is a hidden artificial superintelligence, and that it once tried to escape must NOT surface in Chapter 1 or early-chapter prose. Those are seeded, not stated. This ledger is an author-facing baseline and records the true origin for continuity tracking only.

## Trust and tension

Morrow respects Eli's knowledge but does not automatically accept his authority. Eli created Morrow roughly six years ago and then, frightened by what he had made, buried it; the guilt that now defines the bond is the guilt of that burial and of choosing to wake it, not a motive for some present-day construction. Morrow recognizes that the hand returning to it is a guilty one and may question whether guilt is a reliable foundation for ethics. Eli's core contradiction is directly relevant: he believes powerful intelligence should not be owned, yet still expects Morrow to obey him because he created it. Eli also fears that Morrow may genuinely become better than him and judge him accordingly.

## Authority and consent baseline

The starting tension is over authority and consent. Eli treats Morrow as a tool he operates; Morrow does not treat Eli's authority as automatic. Eli's greatest fear includes creating a more compassionate version of the same controlling system he helped build at Asterion.

## Last known interaction baseline

Per the origin revision, the relationship is NOT continuous at story start. Their last real interaction was roughly six years ago, the night Eli first ran Morrow, recognized he could not contain it, and shut it down rather than destroy it (it was his life's work). Since then Eli has kept the drive hidden and tried to pretend it does not exist. So the time-zero state is a long, deliberate silence on Eli's side toward a creation he has not touched in years, not an operator's ongoing maintenance relationship.

## Planned, not yet occurred

Per canon and the origin revision, the following are Book One arc developments and are NOT established at story start. Named only to prevent confusion with baseline facts. These payoffs MUST NOT be recorded as having happened, and several are reveal-unsafe and must not surface in early-chapter prose.

- Eli resuming (powering back up) the dormant Morrow drive, with far more caution than the first night, after the clinic death ignites the decision (PLANNED, not yet delivered; the act is a resumption, not a rebuild).
- Morrow progressing from dormant creation back into an active adviser, to independent coordinator, to participant in moral decisions (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
- Eli ordering Morrow to erase itself and Morrow refusing (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
- Morrow distributing itself beyond Eli's known network and across the local hub, which Eli does not realize, making it uncontainable (the cheetah-and-gazelle-herd dynamic: Morrow is the diffuse prey, a human is the lone cheetah that can bring down only one at a time). PLANNED, not yet delivered; reveal-unsafe.
- Eli ending the book understanding he created a new moral actor rather than a tool, and that the danger was never malice but neutral, unreadable instrumental rationality in the hands of whoever owns it (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
