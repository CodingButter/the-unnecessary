# Relationship State at Story Start: Eli and Talia

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

## Baseline classification

Technical urgency versus collective legitimacy. A working relationship between neighbors marked by serious, productive conflict over how community decisions get made.

## History to story start

Both Eli and Talia live and work in the same Detroit neighborhood. Talia is the community coordinator and local communications organizer; Eli is the neighborhood's de facto infrastructure technician. They are established as already operating in the same community space at story start.

## Trust and tension

Talia respects Eli's ability but resents his habit of making urgent technical decisions without community consultation. She believes Eli hides political choices inside engineering language. Eli believes Talia sometimes prioritizes process when delay could cause physical harm. Canon frames this conflict as serious and productive and notes potential for emotional closeness but no immediate romance. This baseline records the non-romantic starting state.

## Alliance and debt

A guarded alliance of necessity: both serve the same neighborhood, Talia through governance and organization, Eli through infrastructure. The alliance is real but contested, with an unresolved disagreement about who decides and how, present from time zero.

## Last known interaction baseline

Canon does not fix a single most recent interaction. The relationship is established as ongoing within shared community work up to story start.

## Planned, not yet occurred

Per canon, the following are Book One arc developments and are NOT established at story start. Named only to prevent confusion with baseline facts.

- Talia demanding oversight of Morrow and becoming a central organizer during the containment crisis (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
- Talia preventing Eli from making the decision about Morrow alone (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
- Talia's privately prepared plans to isolate or destroy parts of Morrow are her secret; canon does not state Eli knows of them at story start.
