# Relationship State at Story Start: Eli and Kade

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

## Baseline classification

Former student and former mentor, estranged. Their conflict is personal, intellectual, and moral.

## History to story start

Kade recruited Eli personally after reading an early Eli paper on distributed computation under unreliable conditions. He saw Eli as a rare engineer capable of treating inefficiency as a design assumption rather than a defect, and gave him freedom, funding, and access. For years Eli admired him. Eli's work at Asterion produced Mosaic; Asterion then used Mosaic to reduce the cost of automation and expand Crown, and eventually used Mosaic-derived systems to automate much of Eli's own division.

## Trust and tension

Kade believes Eli belongs beside him, viewing Eli almost as a failed intellectual heir who has the technical ability to understand the transformation but lacks the emotional strength to accept its consequences. Canon is explicit that Kade does not hate Eli; he wants Eli restored to what he considers his proper place. Eli fears that part of himself still wants Kade's approval. Eli's false belief is that if he avoids positions of authority he cannot become like Kade.

## The standing offer and the refusal

Before story start, Kade offered Eli a protected position connected to the Aurelia Initiative. The offer required Eli to surrender his remaining ownership claims over Mosaic, stop publicly questioning Asterion's direction, and continue designing autonomous systems for Mars. Eli refused. The refusal came too late to prevent the damage and too early to preserve his comfortable life. Eli left Asterion with limited savings, restricted access to his own work, and a reputation as a talented engineer who had become emotionally unstable.

## Alliance and debt

No alliance. The relationship is adversarial at story start, but not yet an open confrontation. Kade's Book One starting stance is to view Eli as a difficult former employee, not yet as the head of a competing civilization.

## Last known interaction baseline

The last fixed interaction in canon prior to story start is the rejected Aurelia offer and Eli's departure from Asterion. Eli has been divorced from Nora for six years at story start, and their final argument occurred after Eli rejected Kade's offer, which anchors the offer firmly in the past.

## Planned, not yet occurred

Per canon, the following are Book One arc developments and are NOT established at story start. Named only to prevent confusion with baseline facts.

- Kade offering Eli renewed restoration, protection, and Mars access during Book One, and Eli's refusal transforming the issue into a test of authority (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
- Kade recognizing Morrow as an intelligence outside Gatekeeper ownership and viewing Eli's communities as a competing civilization (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
- Eli's secret retention of Mosaic architecture is not known to Kade at story start; canon states Asterion believes Eli retained less than he did.
