# Relationship State at Story Start: Kade and Mara

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

## Baseline classification

Political allies. Per the relationship map, allies whose relationship collapses when Mara realizes past contribution does not guarantee future inclusion. At story start the alliance is still intact from Mara's perspective; the collapse is a Book One development, not a baseline fact.

## History to story start

As Governor of Michigan, Mara negotiated agreements allowing Asterion and other corporations to assume control over power, transportation, and security systems, and helped create the legal structure protecting Asterion's autonomous facilities and Mars assets. She believed this made her a founding political partner in Aurelia.

## Trust and tension

The defining feature at story start is an asymmetry Mara does not yet recognize. Kade never explicitly promised Mara passage; he allowed her to assume it. Mara begins Book One confident in her future. Her false belief is that the powerful respect contribution; the truth she must learn is that the powerful respect control and personal loyalty, and that contribution without leverage is temporary. From Kade's side, canon does not record personal warmth toward Mara comparable to the friends he intends to bring to Mars; she is a political partner whose inclusion he has never committed to.

## Alliance and debt

Mara believes she is owed a place in Aurelia by virtue of founding contribution. Kade has incurred no explicit debt, having only allowed an assumption to stand. The alliance is therefore real in Mara's understanding and conditional or absent in Kade's intentions.

## Last known interaction baseline

Canon does not fix a single most recent direct interaction at story start. The relationship is established as a standing political partnership rooted in the infrastructure-crisis agreements, with no resolution of the passage question.

## Planned, not yet occurred

Per canon, the following are Book One arc developments and are NOT established at story start. Named only to prevent confusion with baseline facts.

- Mara discovering that less influential people received invitations because of personal relationships (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
- Mara's fear turning into anger and her quiet investigation of the exclusion (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
- Mara becoming connected to a faction considering sabotage (PLANNED, not yet delivered).
- Mara's archived legal documents proving Asterion deliberately encouraged wealthy investors to expect future Mars access are her secret; canon does not state Kade knows she holds them at story start.
