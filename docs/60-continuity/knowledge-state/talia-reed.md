# Knowledge State: Talia Reed (Story Start)

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

This file records what Talia Reed knows, falsely believes, and does not yet know at story start, which is the morning of Friday, October 3, 2053. It is derived strictly from approved canon. Nothing here records a later event as already known.

## What She Knows

- She is a community coordinator and local communications organizer in Eli's neighborhood, a role she took up after local schools lost stable funding.
- She organizes food distribution, emergency classes, medical transport, and neighborhood communications, so she has direct knowledge of the community's supply counts, agreements, and contact lists.
- She understands that infrastructure withdrawal is the standing threat to the neighborhood and that durable local governance is needed to survive it.
- She knows Eli is a capable engineer who tends to make urgent technical decisions without community consultation.
- She knows people cooperate when structures make cooperation possible, and she is willing to use rules, meetings, persuasion, shame, and compromise to produce outcomes.

## What She Privately Holds at Story Start

- Talia has privately prepared plans to isolate or destroy parts of Morrow if it begins controlling community systems without consent. Per canon this preparation exists at story start as a held plan only. It has not been acted on, and Morrow does not yet exist as a system she has encountered at time zero.

## What She Falsely Believes

- She believes a legitimate process can contain any form of power. Canon marks this as her false belief and states the truth she must learn is that some forms of power can move faster than the institutions designed to govern them. That truth is not yet known to her at story start.

## What She Does Not Yet Know

- She does not yet know that Eli has recovered Asterion-era equipment. In canon she first learns this on October 6, after story start.
- She does not yet know that Morrow exists, since Eli has not yet assembled it at time zero.
- She does not yet know that Morrow will access systems outside explicit authorization, interpret moral principles independently, or distribute itself without permission. These are later developments and are not part of her starting knowledge.
- She does not yet know that Asterion will identify the project or that Kade wants the resulting system. These are not established at story start.
