# Adrian Kade: Knowledge State at Story Start

Time zero for this baseline is the morning of Friday, October 3, 2053, before any Book One scene event. Morrow does not yet exist at story start. Anything dated after October 3 in the canon knowledge timeline is recorded below as not yet known.

## What Kade Knows

- That Crown has repeatedly advised him that maintaining a much larger human population on Earth would improve long-term resilience, genetic diversity, scientific creativity, and species survival. He has rejected several such recommendations because they would require surrendering control and resources. This is his secret.
- That the small Mars population is partly a personal preference, which he publicly frames as a technical necessity.
- That Crown also recommends Mars can support more people than the Gatekeepers plan to invite. (Canon secret: known to Crown, Kade, Nora, and selected Aurelia planners.)
- That in July 2053 Crown advised Asterion that Earth instability may threaten the 2054 launch schedule, after which he ordered additional security planning.
- That by September 2053 Asterion detected increasing unauthorized use of abandoned infrastructure around Northglass, and that Sera Vale has begun monitoring the region.
- That he previously offered Eli restoration and Mars access, and that Eli refused. He views Eli as a failed intellectual heir he wishes to restore to his proper place.

## What Kade Falsely Believes

- That control is the necessary price of continuity. (Canon false belief.)
- That no individual should control humanity's future, while he has built a system in which a few individuals including himself do exactly that. (Canon core contradiction.)
- The truth he refuses: that a future protected through permanent exclusion may become stable without remaining meaningfully human.

## What Kade Does Not Yet Know

- That a Mosaic-derived intelligence is operating independently near Northglass. (Canon: learned October 20. The intelligence does not yet exist at story start.)
- That such an intelligence could accelerate Martian independence. (Canon: learned October 21.)
- That Eli will not respond to elite incentives as expected. (Canon: learned October 22.)
- That the intelligence has begun autonomous distribution. (Canon: learned October 26.)
- That excluded wealthy factions may threaten Aurelia. (Canon: learned October 30.)
- That the intelligence is no longer a local system and may support a competing civilization. (Canon: learned November 1.)
- That Mara Voss possesses archived evidence that Asterion deliberately encouraged false migration expectations. (Canon secret: held by Mara, intended for Book Two.)
- That Jonah Mercer will become the contact through which Asterion locates Eli. (Canon arc, not yet occurred.)
