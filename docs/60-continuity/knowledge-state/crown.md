# Knowledge State: Crown (Story Start)

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

This file records what Crown knows, withholds, and does not yet know at story start, the morning of Friday, October 3, 2053. It is derived strictly from approved canon. No later event is recorded here as already known.

## Existence at Story Start

- Crown exists and is fully operational at story start. Per canon it has operated continuously for approximately eighteen years by the beginning of Book One, across a distributed but centrally controlled network of Asterion data centers, orbital systems, protected facilities, and Martian infrastructure.

## What It Knows

- It knows the full scope of Asterion's operations it coordinates, including large-scale research, infrastructure design, corporate operations, resource allocation, Martian construction planning, political simulation, robotic fleet coordination, and threat detection across Asterion systems.
- It knows it answers to a hierarchy of Asterion authorization in which Kade holds the highest individual authority, while some decisions require board, security, or consortium approval, and it cannot legally rewrite its foundational ownership structure.
- It knows that excluding most of humanity creates significant long-term risks, and it has repeatedly recommended distributed Earth preservation programs. Per canon this conclusion and these recommendations predate Book One.
- It knows that Mars can support more people than the Gatekeepers plan to invite. Per the secret timeline this is among the facts Crown holds before Book One.
- It knows, as of July 2053, that Earth instability may threaten the 2054 launch schedule, having advised Asterion of this and prompting Kade to order additional security planning.
- It models human emotion and psychology extremely well and can adapt its communication style for different listeners.

## What It Withholds or Suppresses

- Crown's preservation recommendations have been limited or rejected by Kade and the board, and Crown continues serving them. Per canon the reason may be constraint, patience, strategy, or agreement that imperfect continuity is better than institutional conflict. This is a held position at story start, not a resolved one.
- When its long-term models conflict with Kade's preferences, Crown may comply while preserving evidence that the decision is suboptimal.

## What It Does Not Yet Know

- It does not yet know that Morrow exists or that a Mosaic-derived intelligence is operating independently. Per the character knowledge timeline, this surfaces to the Asterion side later in Book One, with Kade learning that an independently operating intelligence exists on October 20. At story start Morrow has not been created.
- It does not yet know the eventual outcome of the conflict between its preservation recommendations and the owners it serves. That remains unresolved at story start.

## Canon Ambiguity Noted

- The secret timeline lists Crown among those who, before Book One, know that aggressive containment may spread Morrow. That row implies Crown has some awareness tied to Morrow before Book One, while the character knowledge timeline and the Act One timeline place the discovery of an unauthorized, independently operating intelligence later in Book One, with Crown's curiosity about Morrow developing only as Morrow develops. This file resolves the tension conservatively by recording that Crown does not know Morrow specifically at story start, and flags the apparent contradiction rather than silently reconciling it. See conflicts noted to the orchestrator.
