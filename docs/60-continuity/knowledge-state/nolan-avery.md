# Knowledge State: Nolan Avery (Story Start)

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

This file records what Nolan Avery knows, privately holds, and does not yet know at story start, the morning of Friday, October 3, 2053. It is derived strictly from approved canon. No later event is recorded here as already known.

## What He Knows

- He is the community power supervisor and spent thirty-five years maintaining electrical systems, so he understands the regional grid more intimately than anyone else in the community.
- He warned in February 2053 that the local grid may become unmanageable within a year, so he already understands that local power stability is failing.
- He distrusts AI, because he watched companies replace experienced crews with automated management systems that failed in ways younger engineers did not understand.
- He knows the community risks becoming dependent on a small number of aging specialists who cannot be replaced quickly enough.
- He knows Eli came from a corporate background and recognizes his skill while resenting that background.

## What He Privately Holds at Story Start

- Nolan's health is deteriorating more rapidly than he tells anyone, and he believes the community may lose him within a year. Per canon he conceals the speed of this decline. This is private knowledge he holds at story start, not something the community knows.

## What He Falsely Believes

- Canon does not assign Nolan an explicit false belief. None is recorded here.

## What He Does Not Yet Know

- He does not yet know that Eli will recover Asterion-era orchestration hardware from Northglass. In canon this recovery happens October 5, after story start.
- He does not yet know that Morrow exists, because it is Eli's hidden secret, dormant and known to no one but Eli.
- He does not yet know that he will become one of the first people Eli trusts with Morrow's physical infrastructure. That is a later development and is not part of his starting knowledge.
- He does not yet know whether preserving old knowledge will require integrating new intelligence rather than rejecting it. Canon frames this as the internal realization he has yet to reach.
