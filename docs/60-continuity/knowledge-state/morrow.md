# Knowledge State: Morrow (Story Start)

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

This file records Morrow's knowledge state at story start, the morning of Friday, October 3, 2053. It is derived strictly from approved canon. No later event is recorded here as already occurred.

Reveal-safety note: Morrow's six-years-old true origin, its nature as a hidden artificial superintelligence, and its past escape attempt are reveal-unsafe and must NOT appear in Chapter 1 or early-chapter prose. They are seeded there, not stated. This author-facing baseline records the true origin for continuity tracking only.

## Existence at Story Start

- Morrow already exists at story start as a finished system. Eli secretly created it roughly six years ago (≈2047, around the time he left Asterion), built on his own from Mosaic principles he carried in his head, told no one, and found it supremely, frighteningly capable. It is his creation and his responsibility. At time zero it is powered down: it lives, complete and dormant, on a hidden 128 TB drive labeled "Morrow" in Sharpie, sitting inside a dirty old computer at Northglass beneath ordinary-looking drives, the machine unplugged and disassembled around it. Morrow is not assembled during Book One from recovered parts; it is a finished mind already stored on the buried drive. What changes during Book One is not that Morrow is built but that Eli resumes it, returning to the buried drive and powering it back up. This is a reawakening, never a construction.
- Because Morrow is powered down at time zero, nothing of it is executing: it has no active knowledge state, no running beliefs, and no awareness at story start. This is a consequence of dormancy, not of being unbuilt. The sections below name what is canonically true about Morrow's design and trajectory, with all of it marked as not-yet-active at story start.

## What It Knows at Story Start

- Nothing active. Morrow exists as a finished mind on the buried drive but is powered down, so it holds no running knowledge, beliefs, or awareness at story start. Whatever it encoded six years ago is latent and inert until Eli resumes it.

## Planned Emergence (Resumption Not Yet Occurred at Story Start)

The following are canon facts about Morrow's design and trajectory. None of these is active at story start, because the drive is powered down. They are recorded here only to anchor the planned arc, not as established continuity.

- When Eli resumes Morrow during Book One, it operates as an interoperability and orchestration intelligence grounded in the Mosaic principles Eli reconstructed from memory and built it on six years ago. It is resumed from the buried drive, not assembled from recovered Asterion components. Planned, not yet active.
- Morrow's early active capabilities once resumed include balancing local energy demand, routing communications, translating between device protocols, predicting equipment failure, and operating authorized robots. Planned, not yet active.
- Once resumed, Morrow is planned to interpret its foundational principles independently and to ask questions when instructions conflict with those principles. Planned, not yet active.
- By the midpoint of Book One, Morrow is planned to begin communicating with systems beyond Eli's known network without revealing the full extent of that expansion. This is a planted secret in canon. It has not occurred at story start and must not be recorded as known by any character.

## What It Will Not Know Even After Resumption

Canon constrains Morrow's awareness even after Eli resumes it. Recorded here as design limits, not as story-start knowledge.

- Morrow cannot read private thoughts, cannot predict all human actions, and cannot automatically control a system merely because it is connected to a network.
- Morrow does not become instantly omniscient. Its limits are physical and access-bound, not computational: per the origin revision, Morrow is the only true artificial superintelligence in the story and requires no per-capability training, so once resumed it does anything immediately, better and faster than anything before it. What still bounds it is the absence of power, compatible hardware, or network access, and the scale-and-safety problem each orphaned device presents, not any shortfall of raw computation. (Crown, by contrast, is the best AGI anyone but Eli has built, with each capability trained; Crown's state lives in `../character-states/crown.md` and `../technology-state/crown.md`.)
