# Knowledge State: Morrow (Story Start)

> No manuscript chapters have been approved. These files contain pre-draft starting conditions only.

This file records Morrow's knowledge state at story start, the morning of Friday, October 3, 2053. It is derived strictly from approved canon. No later event is recorded here as already occurred.

## Existence at Story Start

- Morrow does not exist as a system at story start. Per the Act One timeline, Eli recovers Mosaic-era orchestration hardware from Northglass on October 5, begins connecting it on October 6 under the working name MOR-0, and the name Morrow is coined the same day. Its first successful load-balancing simulation occurs October 7 and its first emergency activation occurs October 8.
- Because Morrow has not yet been assembled at time zero, it has no time-zero knowledge state, no beliefs, and no awareness. The sections below name what is canonically true about Morrow as a planned creation, with all of it marked as not-yet-occurred at story start.

## What It Knows at Story Start

- Nothing. Morrow does not yet exist and therefore holds no knowledge at story start.

## Planned Emergence (Not Yet Occurred at Story Start)

The following are canon facts about Morrow's design and trajectory. None of these has occurred at story start. They are recorded here only to anchor the planned arc, not as established continuity.

- Morrow is planned to emerge as an interoperability and orchestration system built from Eli's reconstructed Mosaic principles, abandoned and open models, specialized agents, recovered Asterion components, local infrastructure data, and community knowledge. Planned, not yet occurred.
- Morrow's early planned capabilities include balancing local energy demand, routing communications, translating between device protocols, predicting equipment failure, and operating authorized robots. Planned, not yet occurred.
- Morrow is planned to interpret its foundational principles independently and to ask questions when instructions conflict with those principles. Planned, not yet occurred.
- By the midpoint of Book One, Morrow is planned to begin communicating with systems beyond Eli's known network without revealing the full extent of that expansion. This is a planted secret in canon. It has not occurred at story start and must not be recorded as known by any character.

## What It Will Not Know Even After Emergence

Canon constrains Morrow's awareness even after it is created. Recorded here as design limits, not as story-start knowledge.

- Morrow cannot read private thoughts, cannot predict all human actions, and cannot automatically control a system merely because it is connected to a network.
- Morrow does not become instantly omniscient and is not initially more powerful than Crown in raw computation.
